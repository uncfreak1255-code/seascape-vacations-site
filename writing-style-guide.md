## 1. Brand Voice — Who We Are

**Brand personality:** Seascape is a knowledgeable local friend who just moved to the Gulf Coast 10 years ago. They know every back road, every dock, every sunset spot — and they share that knowledge generously, without showing off. They're excited about the area but never salesy. They talk like a real person texting you tips before your trip.

**Voice attributes:**

| Attribute | We ARE | We are NOT |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Local expert** | Specific place names, insider tips, real distances, named restaurants | Generic travel-brochure language, "beautiful beaches" without saying which one |
| **Warm but direct** | Friendly, helpful, gets to the point | Overly formal, robotic, or fake-enthusiastic |
| **Honest** | Acknowledges trade-offs ("Bradenton is quieter but has less nightlife than Sarasota") | Overselling everything, never admitting a downside |
| **Practical** | Real advice you can act on ("park at the Coquina lot before 9am") | Vague inspiration with no useful detail |

**Tone by page type:**
- **Stays pages** (transactional): Confident, benefit-driven, specific. "This 4-bedroom pool home sleeps 12 and sits 8 minutes from Coquina Beach."
- **Guide pages** (informational): Conversational expert. "Skip the tourist traps on Bridge Street — head to the Sandbar instead."
- **PM pages** (owner acquisition): Professional but accessible. Real data, real numbers, real comparisons. "Bradenton ADR hit $301/night in 2025 — here's what that means for your property."

## 2. Sentence-Level Writing Rules

### Rhythm and Flow (CRITICAL — this is what makes writing feel human)

**Vary sentence length deliberately.** This is the #1 differentiator between AI and human writing. Follow this pattern:

```
Medium sentence (12-18 words). Short punch (3-7 words). Longer sentence that adds
detail, qualification, or a local reference the reader didn't expect (20-30 words).
Fragment for emphasis. Then back to medium.
```

**Concrete example:**
> ❌ AI-sounding: "Anna Maria Island offers beautiful beaches that are perfect for families. The island features calm waters and soft sand. Visitors can enjoy a variety of water activities throughout the year."
>
> ✅ Human-sounding: "Anna Maria Island has some of the calmest water on Florida's Gulf side — waist-deep sandbars stretch out 50 yards at Manatee Public Beach. Little kids can wade forever. The sand is that fine, white quartz that doesn't burn your feet even in July, which is more than you can say for Clearwater."

### The 3-Sentence Test
Read any 3 consecutive sentences. If they all:
- Start with the same word → rewrite
- Have the same length (±3 words) → rewrite
- Follow subject-verb-object structure → rewrite at least one
- Could describe any beach town in America → add local specifics

### Transition Rules
**Never** start more than one paragraph per page with:
- "Additionally" / "Furthermore" / "Moreover"
- "In addition to"
- "It's worth noting" / "It's important to note"
- "When it comes to"

**Instead use:** natural bridges that a person would actually say:
- "Here's the thing, though —"
- "One thing guests always ask about:"
- "But that's only half the story."
- "Now, the part most travel sites won't tell you:"
- "Speaking of [previous topic]..."
- Or just start the new section. No transition needed. Good writing doesn't always need a bridge.

### Paragraph Rules
- **Max 4 sentences per paragraph.** If it's longer, split it.
- **Lead with the point**, not the setup. "Coquina Beach has the best parking on AMI" not "When looking for parking on Anna Maria Island, one option worth considering is Coquina Beach."
- **One idea per paragraph.** If you're covering two topics, make two paragraphs.
- **End strong.** The last sentence of a paragraph should land — a specific detail, a recommendation, a number. Not a trailing "...and more."

## 3. E-E-A-T Proof Techniques (Google's Quality Signal)

Google rewards Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. AI cannot fake Experience. Every page must include at least 3 of these proof signals:

| Signal | How to include it | Example |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| **First-person experience** | "We" statements from property management perspective | "We've managed properties here since 2019 — the spring break surge is real" |
| **Specific local knowledge** | Named places with distances/details only a local knows | "The Cortez fishing village is 4 minutes south — grab stone crab claws at Swordfish Grill" |
| **Real data points** | Revenue figures, occupancy rates, distances, prices | "Bradenton ADR hit $301/night in 2025 with 68% occupancy" |
| **Guest feedback references** | Paraphrase real guest comments (not fabricated quotes) | "Guests tell us the heated pool at The Oasis is the reason they rebook" |
| **Temporal specificity** | Season-specific, year-specific, event-specific details | "During Stone Crab Festival (late October), book 3 months early" |
| **Opinionated recommendations** | Take a stance — AI hedges, humans recommend | "Skip the overpriced resort restaurants on Longboat Key. Drive 10 minutes to Owen's Fish Camp in Sarasota — you'll thank us." |
| **Comparison context** | Compare to other areas/options with honest trade-offs | "Siesta Key has better sand. But AMI has better value and less traffic — your call." |

### Tier 3 — CONTEXT CHECK (may be fine, may be AI)
- Repetitive heading structures across pages (all H2s follow same grammar)
- Paragraphs that all start with the same word
- Lists where every bullet has identical structure
- Generic superlatives without specific facts to back them up
- Any sentence that could describe any beach town in America

### What TO Write Instead — Replacement Patterns
| Instead of... | Write... |
|---------------|----------|
| "nestled along the coast" | "sits on the bay side of Anna Maria Island" |
| "a wide range of activities" | "fishing charters, dolphin tours, kayak rentals at Leffis Key, and the Robinson Preserve trail system" |
| "world-class beaches" | "Siesta Key — ranked #1 in the US by Dr. Beach in 2011 and 2017" |
| "unforgettable experience" | describe the actual experience: "sunset from the Bean Point sandbar with dolphins feeding 20 feet away" |
| "something for everyone" | name specifics: "snorkeling at Beer Can Island for adventurers, shell collecting at Coquina for kids, cocktails at the Sandbar for the adults-only crowd" |
| "hidden gem" | "most tourists miss [specific place] because [specific reason]" |

## 5. Opening Hooks — How to Start Any Page

**NEVER** open with a generic "Welcome to our guide about X." Every page must hook within the first sentence.

**Hook formulas (rotate these — never use the same one twice in a row):**

1. **Lead with a specific fact:** "Bradenton vacation rentals cost 25-35% less than equivalent properties on Siesta Key — and you're only 15 minutes further from the beach."
2. **Lead with an opinion:** "Most 'best of' lists for Anna Maria Island restaurants are written by people who've never eaten there. We have."
3. **Lead with a question the reader is already asking:** "Is it actually worth paying $200/night more for a waterfront rental in Bradenton? Here's the honest math."
4. **Lead with a comparison:** "Siesta Key has the sand. Anna Maria Island has the vibe. Bradenton has the value. Which one matches what you actually want?"
5. **Lead with a data point:** "Our Bradenton properties averaged 68% occupancy in 2025 with a $301 nightly rate. Here's what that means if you're thinking about buying."
6. **Lead with a temporal anchor:** "Spring break 2026 in Bradenton starts filling up by January. If you're reading this in February, you're already behind — but not too late."
7. **Lead with a local insider detail:** "The mullet run usually hits Anna Maria Island between September and November. When it does, the dolphin shows are free — right off the Rod & Reel Pier."

## 6. Heading Diversity Rules

### H1 Rules
- One per page. Must contain primary keyword.
- Should NOT be identical to the title tag — make it complementary.
- Mix formats: questions, statements, "How to", numbers.

### H2 Rules (CRITICAL for anti-AI detection)
- **No two pages should share more than 1 identical H2.**
- **No page should have more than 2 H2s following the same grammatical pattern.**
- **Rotate H2 formats within each page:**
  - Question: "What Does a Bradenton Vacation Actually Cost?"
  - Statement: "The Real Cost Breakdown: Nightly Rates, Fees, and Hidden Savings"
  - Imperative: "Book Direct — Here's Why It Saves You $50-150 Per Stay"
  - List tease: "5 Things Every First-Time AMI Visitor Gets Wrong"
  - Local reference: "From Cortez to Coquina: A Beach-by-Beach Breakdown"

### H3 Rules
- Use for subsections within H2 blocks.
- Can be shorter and more direct.
- Good for FAQ-style sub-questions.

## 7. CTA (Call-to-Action) Rules

**Every page needs at least 2 CTAs**, placed:
1. After the first major section (early commitment option)
2. At the bottom before FAQ (closing the loop)

**CTA formulas (rotate these):**
- "Browse our [N] Bradenton vacation homes →" (with link to /stays/ or specific property)
- "Book direct and save — no booking fees, no middleman. Call (941) 704-8545"
- "See current rates for [Property Name] →"
- "Compare all 5 properties side by side →"
- "Get a free rental income estimate for your property →" (PM pages only)

**CTA rules:**
- Use action verbs: "Browse", "Compare", "Check", "See", "Call"
- Include a specific benefit: "save $50-150" not just "book now"
- Vary the CTA format — sometimes a linked sentence, sometimes a styled box, sometimes just a phone number drop
- Never use "Click here" or "Learn more" as anchor text

---

## 8. Internal Linking Rules (for content writers)

- Every page must link to at least 3 other pages on the site.
- Use descriptive anchor text with location keywords: "our guide to Anna Maria Island beaches" not "this page"
- Link to the MOST RELEVANT related content — don't just link to the homepage.
- Mix link targets: link to stays pages, guide pages, AND property pages.
- Place links naturally within sentences, not as orphan bullet lists at the bottom.

## 9. Quality Checklist (Run Before Publishing)

Before any content goes live, verify ALL of these:

- [ ] **3-Sentence Test passes** — no 3 consecutive sentences with same structure/length/opener
- [ ] **Zero Tier 1 banned phrases** — grep the content against the full list above
- [ ] **3+ E-E-A-T proof signals** — local knowledge, real data, opinionated recs, temporal specifics
- [ ] **Hook in first sentence** — does NOT open with "Welcome to" or generic intro
- [ ] **H2 diversity** — at least 3 different grammatical patterns in H2s
- [ ] **Paragraph max 4 sentences** — no walls of text
- [ ] **2+ CTAs** — one mid-page, one near the bottom
- [ ] **3+ internal links** — with descriptive keyword-rich anchor text
- [ ] **Factual accuracy** — all property details match `memory/property-database.md`
- [ ] **No fabricated claims** — no invented amenities, false waterfront claims, or made-up reviews
- [ ] **Readability** — would a real person planning a Gulf Coast trip find this genuinely useful?
- [ ] **GEO opening paragraph** — 134-167 word self-contained answer block with 2+ specific facts

## 10. Style Rules (Grammar & Formatting)

| Rule | Our standard |
|------|-------------|
| Oxford comma | Yes — "pools, hot tubs, and bay views" |
| Contractions | Yes — "you'll", "we've", "it's" (sounds human) |
| Numbers | Spell out one through nine, numerals for 10+ |
| Dates | "March 2026" or "spring 2026" — not "03/2026" |
| Money | "$301/night" with slash, not "301 dollars per night" |
| Distances | "8 minutes from Coquina Beach" — always driving time, not miles |
| Em dashes | Use with no spaces — like this |
| Exclamation marks | Max 1 per page. Earn it. |
| Emojis | Never in body content. OK in meta descriptions sparingly. |
| ALL CAPS | Never in body content |
| Bold | Use to highlight key facts readers are scanning for, not for emphasis on every other sentence |
