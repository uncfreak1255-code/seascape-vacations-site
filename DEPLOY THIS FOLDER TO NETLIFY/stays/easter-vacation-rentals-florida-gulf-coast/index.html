Why the Gulf Coast for Easter

Florida's Gulf Coast — specifically the Bradenton, Anna Maria Island, and Sarasota corridor — has a different energy than the Atlantic side. The water is calmer (no strong currents), the sunsets are on the beach instead of behind you, and the pace is slower. For Easter gatherings, this matters. You want a place where the whole family can relax, not dodge jet skis.

Our properties are built for group holidays. The Oasis sleeps 16 with a massive pool, two hot tubs, and a backyard mini golf course — keep the kids entertained for hours. Dockside Dreams gives you waterfront dock access for fishing or just watching dolphins cruise past. Easter Week Activities Worth Your Time

Anna Maria Island hosts an annual Easter egg hunt at Bayfront Park — it's free, chaotic in the best way, and the kids talk about it for months. The Anna Maria Island area guide has the full rundown on island events. For a more structured outing, the Bishop Museum of Science and Nature in Bradenton runs a spring wildlife exhibit that's genuinely interesting for adults and kids alike.

On the water side, April is prime tarpon season. Charter boats out of Cortez Village and Longboat Key run half-day trips that are perfect for a father-son Easter morning. The Manatee River warms up enough by early April that kayaking through Robinson Preserve is comfortable — and you'll likely spot manatees, osprey, and the occasional eagle.

Weather and What to Pack

Early April on the Gulf Coast is the sweet spot. Expect daytime temps of 78-84°F, overnight lows around 65°F, and water temperatures that are swimmable without the "gasp-when-you-wade-in" moment. Rain is possible but brief — afternoon storms blow through in 20 minutes and leave behind the most dramatic sunsets you've ever seen.

Pack reef-safe sunscreen (Manatee County takes marine conservation seriously), a light jacket for evening boat rides, and water shoes if you plan to kayak through the mangroves. Our homes provide beach chairs, umbrellas, and coolers, so leave those at home.

Easter Dining — Where Locals Actually Go

Here's what nobody tells you about Easter brunch on the Gulf Coast: every waterfront restaurant within 20 miles is booked by mid-March. The Sandbar on Anna Maria Island does a waterfront spread worth the drive — but show up by 9:30am or you're looking at a 90-minute wait. They don't take reservations for brunch.

Closer to our Bradenton properties, Sage Biscuit Cafe on Manatee Avenue opens at 7am. No reservations. The line moves fast, though, and their shrimp and grits are the real reason people drive past five other breakfast spots to get here. For something more upscale, the Bradenton area guide covers our full restaurant picks.

Easter dinner is a different game. Owen's Fish Camp in downtown Sarasota has porch seating for 8 and the grouper is fresh off the boat. Reserve at least two weeks out for Easter Sunday. Staying in Bradenton? Pier 22 on the Manatee River does sunset views and a raw bar — solid pick for groups of 6 or more.

Two more you should know about. Star Fish Company in Cortez Village — 10 minutes from any of our properties — is the most authentic (and affordable) seafood shack on this stretch of coast. And Anna Maria Oyster Bar at Landside: fried grouper sandwich, side of cheese grits. That's the order. Don't overthink it.

A Sample Easter Week Itinerary (So You Don't Waste a Day)

Most families lose their first day to "figuring out the area." Don't be that family. Here's a rough week, assuming you check in Monday and Easter Sunday is your last full day:

Monday–Tuesday: Beach days at Coquina Beach (best parking on AMI, free, restrooms) or Manatee Public Beach (calmer water, better for small kids). Grocery run at Publix on Cortez Road — stock up for the week. Evening pool time at your rental.

Wednesday: Robinson Preserve in the morning — 4 miles of kayak trails through mangroves, free launch. Afternoon at the Bishop Museum with the kids. Dinner at Star Fish Company in Cortez while the sun drops.

Thursday–Friday: Half-day fishing charter out of Cortez (Capt. Zach's Fishing Charters runs family-friendly trips, $600 for up to 6 people). Things to do in Bradenton has more ideas if you need them. Friday evening, drive 25 minutes to downtown Sarasota for St. Armands Circle shopping and dinner.

Saturday: Bean Point at the north tip of Anna Maria Island — the sandbar at low tide stretches 100 yards out. This is the photo op. Bring a cooler. Saturday night, fire up the grill at your rental — every property has an outdoor dining setup.

Easter Sunday: Sunrise service on the beach (Manatee Public Beach and Holmes Beach both host them). Easter egg hunt at Bayfront Park mid-morning. Brunch at the Sandbar or cook Easter dinner in your rental's full kitchen — you've got the counter space and the oven for it.

Easter Rental Questions

When is Easter 2026?

Easter Sunday is April 5, 2026. Most families book vacation rentals for the full week of March 30 through April 6. Gulf Coast availability drops significantly by February for Easter week, so early booking is recommended.

How warm is the Gulf Coast during Easter week?

Early April brings daytime highs of 78-84°F and Gulf water temperatures around 76°F. The water is comfortably swimmable, and afternoon rain showers are brief (usually 15-20 minutes). It is one of the best times of year for beach weather on Florida's west coast.

Are there Easter events near Anna Maria Island?

Yes. Anna Maria Island hosts a free community Easter egg hunt at Bayfront Park. The Bishop Museum of Science and Nature in Bradenton runs spring wildlife programs. Many local churches host sunrise services on the beach. Check the Anna Maria Island Chamber website for the latest event listings.
