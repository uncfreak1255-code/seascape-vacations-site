Common Questions

Need-to-Know: Downtown Sarasota Rentals

Can I walk to restaurants from your downtown-area Sarasota rental?

Our Sarasota property is a short drive from downtown's best restaurants and attractions. While not directly in the walkable downtown core, you're close enough that getting to Palm Avenue restaurants, Main Street shops, and the bayfront takes just minutes by car.

What's special about St. Armands Circle?

St. Armands Circle is an iconic upscale shopping and dining district on St. Armands Key, connected to Lido Key and Sarasota by the Ringling Bridge. It features boutique clothing stores, jewelry shops, art galleries, and restaurants ranging from casual to fine dining — all arranged around a beautiful circular park.

Is downtown Sarasota good for couples?

Downtown Sarasota is one of the most romantic destinations on the Gulf Coast. Outstanding fine dining, intimate theaters, sunset walks along the bayfront, gallery strolls, wine bars, and nearby beaches create the perfect couples' experience. The area's sophistication sets it apart from typical beach towns.

What museums and cultural attractions are in downtown Sarasota?

Downtown Sarasota is packed with culture: the John and Mable Ringling Museum of Art, Marie Selby Botanical Gardens, Sarasota Art Museum, Van Wezel Performing Arts Hall, Asolo Repertory Theatre, Sarasota Opera House, Florida Studio Theatre, and numerous private galleries.

How far is downtown Sarasota from the beaches?

Lido Beach is about 10–15 minutes from downtown Sarasota across the Ringling Bridge. Siesta Key Beach is 15–20 minutes south. Anna Maria Island beaches are about 30 minutes north. You have your pick of Gulf Coast beaches within easy driving distance.

What events happen in downtown Sarasota?

Sarasota has a packed events calendar: First Friday Gallery Walks, the Sarasota Film Festival, Ringling International Arts Festival, weekly farmer's markets at the Marina, outdoor concerts at Bayfront Park, seasonal theater productions, and food/wine festivals throughout the year.
