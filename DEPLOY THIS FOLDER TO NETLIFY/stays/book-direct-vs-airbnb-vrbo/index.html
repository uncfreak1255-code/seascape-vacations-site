Booking Direct — Your Questions Answered

How much does Airbnb charge in service fees?

As of late 2025, Airbnb switched to a host-only fee model. Hosts now pay 15.5% to Airbnb, which gets baked into the nightly rate guests see. Before this change, guests paid 14-16% on top of the listed price. Either way, that cost gets passed to you — the guest. When you book direct with the property owner, that 15.5% platform cut disappears entirely.

Is it safe to book a vacation rental directly with the owner?

Yes — with a reputable management company. Look for a real website with reviews, a phone number you can call, and a physical address. Seascape Vacations manages 5 luxury homes in Bradenton and Sarasota, Florida. We answer our phone, respond to texts, and have 4.8+ star average reviews across all properties. You get the same property either way — just without the platform markup.

Why are vacation rentals cheaper when you book direct?

Platforms like Airbnb and VRBO take 15-20% of every booking in combined host and guest fees. When you book direct, that money either stays in your pocket or the owner can reinvest it in the property. On a $2,500 week-long Gulf Coast rental, you're looking at $180-$350 in savings by cutting out the middleman.

Do I still get cancellation protection when booking direct?

Most professional vacation rental managers — including Seascape Vacations — offer their own cancellation policies. Ours is actually more flexible than Airbnb's standard policy for many booking types. We also recommend travel insurance for hurricane season stays (June-November), which costs a fraction of what you save by booking direct.
