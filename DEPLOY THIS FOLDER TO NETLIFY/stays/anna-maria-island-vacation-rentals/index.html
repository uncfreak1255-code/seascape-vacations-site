What Everyone Wants to Know About Renting on AMI

Q: Do I really need to stay ON the island?

Short answer — no. And here's why most of our repeat guests prefer it that way. Anna Maria Island is 7 miles long and about half a mile wide. The vacation rentals on-island are mostly small cottages and condos crammed together. You're paying island-premium prices for a place where you can hear your neighbor's TV through the wall.

Our Bradenton properties are 12-15 minutes from AMI beaches. The difference? You get a 4 or 5 bedroom house with a heated pool, a full kitchen, and enough room for your entire family to spread out. The math works: save 30-40% on nightly rates, spend that money on boat rentals and fresh grouper at the Sandbar instead.

Q: What about parking on Anna Maria Island?

This is the question nobody thinks to ask until they're circling Manatee Public Beach at 11 AM on a Saturday. Parking on AMI is genuinely terrible during season — we're talking 30+ minute waits for a spot. Our guests drive over, park once, and have the whole beach to themselves by 9 AM while day-trippers are still circling.

The Beaches Worth Knowing About

AMI has three communities, each with its own personality. Anna Maria City at the north end has Bean Point — arguably the best beach on Florida's Gulf Coast if you like it quiet. The walk out to the point takes about 10 minutes through sea oats and Australian pines, and on a Tuesday morning in May, you might be the only person there.

Holmes Beach in the middle is where most visitors end up. Manatee Public Beach has restrooms, a snack bar, and the easiest parking (which is still not easy). The Gulf water here is shallow for a long way out — perfect for little kids. The Feast Restaurant on Marina Drive does a crab cake Benedict that's become a mandatory stop for our guests.

Coquina Beach at the south end connects to Bradenton Beach via a walking path. It has the largest parking lot on the island and a great picnic area under the Australian pines. The Tide Tables restaurant sits right at the entrance to Cortez — order the smoked fish dip and a local IPA while watching fishing boats come in.

Getting Around — The Real Logistics

The Anna Maria Island Trolley is free and runs every 20 minutes during season. It connects the entire island from Bean Point to Coquina Beach, so once you're on AMI, you don't need your car. From our Bradenton properties, the drive to the island is 12-15 minutes over the Manatee Avenue bridge — zero traffic at 8 AM, moderate by 10, and you'll want to avoid 2-4 PM on weekends.

Off-island, you're 10 minutes from downtown Bradenton's Riverwalk (great for evening walks), 25 minutes from UTC Mall in Sarasota, and 35 minutes from the Ringling Museum. Tampa airport is about 50 minutes; Sarasota-Bradenton airport is 20.

Common Questions

Anna Maria Island Rental Questions

How far are your rentals from Anna Maria Island beaches?

Our 5 vacation homes in Bradenton and Sarasota are 12-20 minutes from AMI beaches. You get larger homes with private pools at 30-40% less than on-island rentals — plus easier parking and more space.

What's the best beach on Anna Maria Island?

Bean Point at the north tip is the locals' pick — less crowded, incredible shelling, and the best sunset views on the island. Manatee Public Beach in Holmes Beach is best for families with restrooms and a snack bar. Coquina Beach at the south end has the most parking.

Can I walk to the beach from your vacation rentals?

Our properties are a short drive from AMI beaches, not walking distance. That's by design — staying off-island means you get 4-5 bedroom homes with heated pools, game rooms, and resort-style amenities at rates 30-40% lower than comparable on-island rentals.

When is the cheapest time to visit Anna Maria Island?

September and October offer the lowest rates — 40-50% less than peak winter season. The water is still warm (mid-80s), beaches are empty, and you'll have restaurants to yourself. May and early June are also good value before summer family travel picks up.

Do you allow pets at your Anna Maria Island area rentals?

Pet policies vary by property. Contact us directly and we'll match you with a pet-friendly home. Several of our properties welcome dogs with fenced yards and easy access to dog-friendly beaches on AMI.
