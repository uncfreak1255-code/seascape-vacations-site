Anna Maria Island is a 7-mile barrier island off Florida’s Gulf Coast, and it’s the kind of place where you still see dolphins from the Rod & Reel Pier and locals wave at you from golf carts on Pine Avenue. Bean Point sunsets are legitimately some of the best on the Gulf. Bridge Street has that old-Florida charm — live music, small shops, a fishing pier where kids catch pinfish all afternoon. The problem? Staying on the island during peak season can run $400–$800+ a night for a basic cottage with street parking and no pool.

That’s why so many returning guests stay in Bradenton instead. You’re across the Palma Sola Causeway in 10–15 minutes, but your nightly rate drops by half and your property upgrades dramatically. We’re talking private heated pools, hot tubs, waterfront docks, game rooms — the kind of space that actually makes a vacation feel like a vacation. Plus, you skip the island parking headaches entirely.

Here’s the routine our guests swear by: grab breakfast at the Donut Experiment on Pine Ave, spend the morning at Holmes Beach, then come home to your private pool by lunch. Hit up Anna Maria Oyster Bar on the way back (the grouper sandwich is the move), and watch the sunset from your own backyard with a drink in hand. That’s the play.

Why Bradenton Is the Smarter AMI Base Camp

Anna Maria Island has a 3-story building height limit. That keeps it charming, but it also means limited inventory and sky-high prices during season. Bradenton sits right across the bay with newer, larger homes that have room for real amenities.

Here’s what we mean by “real amenities”: The Oasis has a mini golf course in the backyard, two hot tubs, and a pool big enough for your entire extended family. Dockside Dreams has a 40-foot boat dock on a deep-water canal that connects to Tampa Bay. Try finding that on AMI for under $1,000 a night. You won’t.

The Palma Sola Causeway drive is actually one of the highlights — pelicans diving, people fishing off the bridge, and that first glimpse of the Gulf when you crest the top. Some guests tell us the drive over is part of the experience.

What to Actually Do on Anna Maria Island

Skip the tourist trap restaurants on Gulf Drive. Seriously.

Instead, start your morning at the Donut Experiment on Pine Avenue — they do made-to-order cake donuts with wild toppings and the line moves fast. Walk Pine Ave after, it’s got local boutiques, art galleries, and a vibe that feels more Key West than corporate Florida. For lunch, Duffy’s Tavern has been around since 1972 and the burgers are still great. Or hit the Sandbar on Spring Avenue for waterfront dining where your kids can play on the beach while you eat.

Bean Point at the island’s north tip is where locals go. Fewer people, better shelling, and the sunset views are unreal. Bring a chair and a cooler. Holmes Beach is the main family beach — wide, calm, and lifeguards on weekends during season.

For fishing, the Rod & Reel Pier in Anna Maria City is a landmark. $5 to fish, no license needed on the pier, and you’ll see people pulling in mackerel and sheepshead all day. The pier restaurant serves cold beer and fried grouper — you can eat while you fish. Hard to beat that.

Pro tip: the free AMI island trolley runs the entire length of the island. Park once at Coquina Beach (south end, free lot) and ride the trolley north to Pine Avenue and back. Saves you the headache of finding parking in Anna Maria City during season.

Common Questions

Anna Maria Island Vacation Rental FAQs

How far are your vacation rentals from Anna Maria Island?

Our vacation homes in Bradenton are 10–20 minutes from Anna Maria Island by car. Dockside Dreams and River House are closest at about 12 minutes via Manatee Avenue (SR 64) to Holmes Beach. The Oasis and Bradenton Pool Home are roughly 18–20 minutes. It’s a straight shot across the Palma Sola Causeway — no highway driving, just a scenic bridge ride over the bay.

Is it better to stay on Anna Maria Island or in Bradenton?

Depends on your priorities. On-island rentals put you within walking distance of the beach, but you’ll pay $400–$800+/night for smaller homes with street parking and no pool. Our Bradenton homes start at $250/night, every property has a private pool, and you get 2–3x the space. Most of our guests say the 12-minute drive to the beach is worth it for the extra amenities and savings.

What are the best beaches near your vacation homes?

Holmes Beach is the closest and best for families — wide sand, gentle waves, lifeguards on duty. Bean Point at the north tip of AMI is where locals go for sunset walks and shelling (fewer crowds, more wildlife). Manatee Public Beach has the best facilities with showers, restrooms, and a cafe. Coquina Beach at the south end is great for kayak launching and has a bayside beach too.

Do I need a car when staying near Anna Maria Island?

Yes, you’ll want a car. Our homes are on the mainland in Bradenton, so you’ll drive to the island. Once you’re on AMI, though, the free island trolley runs the full length from Anna Maria City to Coquina Beach. Many guests drive over in the morning, park once, and trolley around all day. A few of our properties also have dock access for boating to the island if you’d rather skip traffic entirely.

When is the best time to visit Anna Maria Island?

March through May is the sweet spot — water temps hit the mid-70s, humidity is manageable, and the spring break crowds thin out by mid-April. October and November are the hidden gem months: prices drop, the water is still warm from summer, and you’ll have the beaches practically to yourself. Peak season (December through March) has the best weather but highest rates.
