Need More? Travel Insurance Options Abound

Why Travel Insurance Matters

Vacation rentals require advance booking and deposits. Life is unpredictable—illness, family emergencies, flight cancellations, and weather can all impact your plans. Travel insurance protects your investment.

What to Look For:

  • Trip Cancellation: Reimburses non-refundable costs if you cancel for covered reasons
  • Cancel for Any Reason (CFAR): Most flexible option—cancel for any reason and receive 50-75% reimbursement
  • Trip Interruption: If you must leave early due to covered events
  • Medical Coverage: Important for international visitors
  • Hurricane/Weather: Specific coverage for weather-related cancellations

Our Recommendation: We recommend purchasing travel insurance within 14 days of booking to qualify for pre-existing condition waivers and CFAR options.

Trusted Providers: Allianz Travel, Travelex, and Squaremouth are popular options. Squaremouth.com is excellent for comparing policies.

Your Travel Insurance Home Is Ready

Florida's hurricane season runs June through November, peaking in September and October. If you're booking during these months, travel insurance is honestly essential—not optional. We've had guests whose flights were cancelled or delayed by days, forcing them to miss the first part of their stay. With the right coverage, you're reimbursed. Without it, you lose that money.

That said, don't let hurricane season keep you away. September and October are shoulder season—fewer tourists, better rates, and the weather is usually gorgeous. Just insure yourself against the possibility. If a hurricane does force evacuation, you want coverage that reimburses cancelled portions of your stay.

Watch the National Hurricane Center (nhc.noaa.gov) about 2 weeks before your trip. Typically, if a major system is 10+ days out, conditions can still shift. Within 5 days, you'll have much better certainty.

How Much Does Travel Insurance Cost?

Travel insurance premiums usually run 5–12% of your total trip cost, depending on your coverage level and when you buy it.
