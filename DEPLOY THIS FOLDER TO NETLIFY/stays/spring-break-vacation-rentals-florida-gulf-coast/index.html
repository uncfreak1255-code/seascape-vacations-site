Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to book a Florida Gulf Coast spring break rental?

Book 3–4 months in advance for spring break weeks (March 7–April 18, 2026). Peak weeks — mid-March through first week of April — book out by January. Waiting until February means limited inventory and 15–25% higher rates. If you're reading this in March, check availability immediately — most quality direct-book properties have only 1–2 spring break weeks remaining.

How much does a Gulf Coast spring break vacation rental cost in 2026?

Expect $350–$650/night for a 4-bedroom Gulf Coast home sleeping 8–10. Homes sleeping 12–16 run $550–$950/night. Booking direct through a boutique property manager like Seascape Vacations saves 10–15% vs. Vrbo or Airbnb platform fees. A 7-night stay at a 4-bedroom saves roughly $350–$650 when you cut out OTA service fees.

Is Anna Maria Island or Sarasota better for spring break with kids?

Anna Maria Island is better for families: car-free village feel, calm shallow water at Manatee Beach, free trolley system, and a 7-mile island that's easy to navigate. Children under 8 can wade 50 feet out and still be in knee-deep water on the Gulf side. Sarasota suits mixed groups wanting both beach and city — Siesta Key Beach, downtown restaurants, and Ringling Museum access.

What Gulf Coast airport is closest to Bradenton and Anna Maria Island rentals?

Tampa International (TPA) is the best option for most travelers: 45 minutes from Bradenton, 55 minutes from the north end of Anna Maria Island, and served by all major carriers. Sarasota/Bradenton International (SRQ) is 20 minutes from downtown Bradenton and 30 minutes from AMI — fewer direct routes but zero baggage-claim stress. See our airport comparison table below.
