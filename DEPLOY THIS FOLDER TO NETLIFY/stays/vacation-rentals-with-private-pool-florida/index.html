Vacation Rentals with Private Pools in Florida

Your own heated pool, your own schedule. No hotel crowds, no towel reservations, no nonsense.

Every Seascape Vacations property comes with a heated private pool — that's not an upsell, it's standard. All 5 of our homes in Bradenton and Sarasota have screened-in pool areas with pool heaters that keep the water in the low-to-mid 80s even in January. No sharing with hotel strangers. No fighting for pool chairs at 7 AM. No lifeguard whistles. Just your group, your pool, on your schedule.

Here's what actually matters when you're comparing private pool rentals in Florida: pool size, heating capability, screen enclosure quality, and what surrounds the pool. A 10x20 unheated pool behind a privacy fence is a very different experience than a 15x30 heated pool with hot tub, covered lanai, and waterfront views. Our properties lean heavily toward the latter.

Pool Specs That Actually Matter for Your Trip

Pool temperature is the #1 factor guests underestimate. Florida's air temperature might be 78°F in February, but an unheated pool will be in the low 60s — cold enough that nobody's swimming. Our pools run electric heat pumps that maintain water between 82-86°F year-round. That sounds like a small detail until you're the family whose vacation revolves around pool time and your rental's "pool" is essentially a cold bath.

Pool size matters for groups. The Oasis has the largest pool in our lineup — big enough for actual lap swimming and water games with 16 people. Dockside Dreams and River House have mid-sized pools with attached spas. Even our smallest pool at Bradenton Pool Home is significantly larger than a standard hotel pool, and you don't share it with 200 other guests.

Private Pool vs. Hotel Pool — The Real Comparison

Hotels advertise their pools as a feature. But here's the reality of a resort pool in February: 150 people around a 4,000 sq ft pool deck, towels claimed by 7 AM, kids screaming, pool bar overcharging for watered-down drinks, and a lifeguard blowing a whistle every 4 minutes. It's not relaxation — it's a crowd management exercise.

A private pool vacation rental flips that completely. Your family of 8 has a pool bigger than many hotel pools, heated to your preferred temperature, surrounded by a screened lanai with comfortable seating. You can swim at midnight if you want. The kids can be as loud as they want. You can float with a drink that you made in your own kitchen for a quarter of the bar price. It's not even close.

The Cost Math Actually Favors Private Pools

Here's the math people don't do: A family of 6 booking two hotel rooms at $250/night spends $500/night with no pool privacy, no kitchen, and cramped quarters. That same family books a 4-bedroom Seascape home for $400/night — private pool, full kitchen (saving $100+/day on restaurants), washer/dryer, and 2,000+ sq ft of living space. The pool home is cheaper AND better.

What We Do Between Guests — Pool Maintenance Standards

This part isn't glamorous but it matters. Between every guest stay, our pool maintenance team tests and adjusts chemical levels, checks the heater and pump operation, cleans the pool surface and walls, inspects the screen enclosure for any holes or damage, and ensures all pool furniture is clean and in good condition. The pool is guest-ready when you arrive — not still being cleaned while you're checking in like some rentals.

During longer stays (7+ nights), we schedule a mid-stay pool check to ensure water chemistry stays balanced. Florida sun and heavy use can shift pH and chlorine levels faster than you'd expect. Our guests don't need to think about pool maintenance — that's our job.

Pool Tip: Run the pool heater to 84°F for daytime swimming and bump it to 86°F if you want warm evening swims. The heat pump takes about 2-3 hours to raise the temp 2 degrees, so plan ahead. We leave instructions at each property for heater controls — it's simpler than it sounds.

Night Swimming & Florida Evenings

One of the most underrated parts of a private pool: night swimming. After a day at the beach or exploring Bradenton, coming home to a lit-up pool with warm water and no crowds is pure vacation bliss. Most of our properties have underwater pool lights and landscape lighting around the lanai that create a resort-like atmosphere after dark.

The Oasis takes this to another level with color-changing LED pool lights, string lights across the lanai, and the two hot tubs lit up against the mini golf backdrop. It looks like a private resort. Guests who stay there specifically mention the nighttime pool vibe as a highlight — especially groups celebrating birthdays, anniversaries, or bachelorette parties.

Common Questions

Private Pool Questions

Are the pools heated year-round?

Yes, every Seascape Vacations pool is heated year-round using electric heat pumps. Water temperature is maintained between 82-86°F regardless of season. Even in January, when unheated Florida pools drop to the 60s, our pools are warm and swimmable. Heater controls are available at each property.

How big are the private pools?

Pool sizes range from our standard 10x20 at Bradenton Pool Home to the large 15x30 at The Oasis. Dockside Dreams, River House, and Sarasota Luxe have medium pools (12x22 to 12x24). All pools are significantly larger than typical hotel pools, and you don't share them with other guests.

Is the pool area screened in?

Yes, every pool at our properties is within a screened lanai enclosure. The screening blocks mosquitoes, no-see-ums, and debris while allowing full airflow and visibility. This is essential for comfortable outdoor living in Florida, especially during dawn and dusk when biting insects are most active.

How often is the pool cleaned?

Pools are professionally serviced between every guest checkout, including chemical balancing, surface cleaning, filter checks, and equipment inspection. For stays of 7+ nights, we schedule a complimentary mid-stay pool maintenance visit. You never need to handle pool chemicals or maintenance during your stay.

Can we swim at night?

Absolutely — the pool is yours 24/7. All our properties have pool lighting for safe night swimming. The Oasis features color-changing LED pool lights. There are no pool hours or restrictions. Many guests say evening pool sessions after a day at the beach are their favorite part of the trip.

Is there a pool fence for young children?

Several properties have removable pool safety fences available upon request. The screened lanai enclosure provides an additional safety barrier around the entire pool area. Please let us know if you need a pool fence when booking, and we'll have it installed before your arrival. Children should always be supervised near water.
