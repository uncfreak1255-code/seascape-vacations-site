First time I drove down Gulf Drive in Holmes Beach, I almost missed it. No high-rises. No chain restaurants with neon signs fighting for attention. Just low-slung shops with hand-painted signs, a couple of guys selling fresh shrimp from a cooler in a parking lot, and palm trees that looked like they’d been there since before the bridge was built. That old-Florida feel? Holmes Beach still has it. And that’s exactly why people keep coming back.

Holmes Beach sits right in the middle of Anna Maria Island — the biggest of the three towns on the island, sandwiched between the city of Anna Maria at the north tip and Bradenton Beach to the south. It’s where most of the restaurants, shops, and public beach access are concentrated. Manatee Public Beach alone draws thousands of visitors a week with its wide sand, free parking (if you get there early), and lifeguards on duty.

What Makes Holmes Beach Worth the Trip

Anna Maria Island has three towns, but Holmes Beach is where most of the action happens. Not “action” like South Beach or Clearwater — more like a place where you can grab smoked ribs at Mr. Bones BBQ, walk a block to the beach, and not hear a single car horn all day.

If you want a proper dinner out, Beach Bistro on Gulf Drive is the real deal. White tablecloths, Gulf views, a wine list that’ll make you stay longer than planned. It’s one of the top-rated restaurants on the entire Gulf Coast, and it’s tiny — reserve ahead or don’t bother.

For the opposite vibe, the Rod & Reel Pier at the north end of the island is where you go with flip-flops and a fishing rod. The pier restaurant serves solid grouper sandwiches and cold beer right over the water. Pelicans everywhere. Kids love it.

Manatee Public Beach is the biggest beach access point in Holmes Beach. Lifeguards, restrooms, a concession stand, and that big parking lot everyone fights over. But here’s a local tip: the beach access at 73rd Street is way less crowded, and the sand is just as good. No facilities, though — bring your own cooler.

Worth the walk north: Bean Point at the very tip of the island. It’s where Tampa Bay meets the Gulf, the current rips through, and you’ll find some of the best shelling on the island. Not great for swimming (the current’s strong), but it’s the kind of spot that makes you feel like you found something everyone else missed.

The shops along Gulf Drive are mostly local — surf shops, a couple of galleries, some places selling shells and tourist stuff. Not fancy. That’s the point. Holmes Beach hasn’t been overrun yet, and the locals intend to keep it that way.

Getting to Holmes Beach from Our Properties

Two bridges connect the mainland to Anna Maria Island. Manatee Avenue (SR 64) drops you right into Holmes Beach — it’s the most direct route. Cortez Road (SR 684) brings you to Bradenton Beach at the south end, then you drive north through the island. Both work fine.

Pro tip: avoid Manatee Avenue around 5pm on weekdays. The bridge backs up with commuter traffic heading off the island. Cortez tends to move better in the afternoon. On weekend mornings, both are fine — just go early if you want beach parking.

Common Questions

Holmes Beach Questions We Hear All the Time

Is Holmes Beach the same as Anna Maria Island?

Holmes Beach is one of three cities on Anna Maria Island, along with Anna Maria (at the north end) and Bradenton Beach (at the south end). It’s the largest of the three and sits in the middle of the island. Most of the restaurants, shops, and public beach access are here. When people say they’re heading to Anna Maria Island, they usually end up in Holmes Beach.

Where do I park at Holmes Beach?

The biggest public lot is at Manatee Public Beach on Gulf Drive — around 200 free spaces. Arrive before 10am on weekends and holidays or you’ll be circling. There are also metered spots along Gulf Drive and smaller public lots near 66th Street and 73rd Street. Staying at a Seascape property? You’ve got private parking at the house and just drive the 15 minutes over.

What are the best restaurants in Holmes Beach?

Beach Bistro is the fine-dining standout on the island, right on the Gulf. Mr. Bones BBQ is a local favorite for casual eats — legit smoked ribs. Skinny’s Place has been around forever and does a great burger with a cold beer. The Rod & Reel Pier restaurant at the north end has waterfront seating you can’t beat, and the grouper sandwich is worth the drive up.

Can I bring a boat to Holmes Beach?

Kingfish Boat Ramp on the Manatee Avenue causeway is the closest launch to Holmes Beach — gets crowded early on weekends, so arrive at dawn or skip it. Coquina Beach boat ramp at the south end works too. Or stay at our Dockside Dreams property, which has a private dock on a deep-water canal with direct Gulf access. Keep your boat at the house and skip the ramp entirely. Check out our fishing vacation rentals for more on the boating angle.
