Vacation Rentals with Screened Lanais in Florida

Bug-free outdoor living with pool views, sunsets, and Gulf Coast breezes — the lanai is where Florida happens.

Updated March 2026

By Seascape Vacations Team | Last Updated: March 2026

If you've never experienced a Florida screened lanai, here's what you're missing: an outdoor room that lets you enjoy the weather year-round without fighting mosquitoes, no-see-ums, or lovebugs. Every Seascape Vacations property in Bradenton and Sarasota features a screened pool enclosure and lanai area, because honestly, a Florida vacation rental without one is doing it wrong.

The lanai (pronounced luh-NYE, not LAH-nai — you'll hear locals correct tourists on this) is where you'll spend most of your time. Morning coffee watching the canal. Afternoon pool sessions with the kids safely enclosed. Evening cocktails while the sunset paints the sky orange and pink through the screen. It's the reason Floridians build their homes around outdoor space rather than treating the backyard as an afterthought.

What Makes a Great Screened Lanai (Not All Are Equal)

A lanai is only as good as its layout. The best ones wrap around the pool and include distinct zones — a dining area with a table big enough for the whole group, a lounge area with comfortable seating, and a cooking zone if there's an outdoor kitchen. Our properties have been designed with this in mind. The Oasis, for example, has a lanai large enough for 16 people to hang out without feeling crowded, with two hot tubs and a pool all under screen.

Screen quality matters more than most guests realize. Cheap screens develop holes within a season and let in the exact bugs you're trying to avoid. Our properties use 18x14 mesh fiberglass screening — tight enough to block no-see-ums (the tiny biting flies that are far worse than mosquitoes) while still allowing good airflow. We inspect and maintain screens before every guest turnover.

The No-See-Um Factor — This Is Why Screens Matter

Visitors from up north underestimate no-see-ums until their first unscreened evening. These sand flies are tiny enough to pass through standard window screens and their bites itch for days. Bradenton's proximity to mangrove habitat means no-see-um populations spike at dawn and dusk, especially between March and October. A properly screened lanai is the difference between a relaxing evening outside and retreating indoors covered in welts.

Lanai Living by Season — What to Expect

Winter (December through March): Peak lanai season. Daytime temps in the 70s, low humidity, virtually no bugs. You'll eat every meal outside. Heated pools and hot tubs make evenings perfect. This is when northern guests realize why Floridians are obsessed with outdoor living — and why winter bookings fill up fast.

Spring (April through May): Warming up, still comfortable. Lovebug season hits twice a year (April and September) — they're harmless but annoying, and the screen keeps them out of your face. Pool water warms naturally to the low 80s. Thunderstorms start rolling in late afternoon, and watching storms from a dry screened lanai is genuinely one of the best Florida experiences.

Summer (June through August): Hot and humid, no sugarcoating it. But the lanai creates shade, and being near the pool means you cycle between swimming and lounging. Mornings and evenings are beautiful under screen. This is when the ceiling fans on the lanai earn their keep — good airflow makes 85°F feel surprisingly comfortable in shade.

Fall (September through November): Humidity drops, bugs thin out, and the lanai becomes the default living room again. Water temps stay warm through October. Second lovebug season in September. By November, you're back to winter-style perfection.

Why Florida Homes Are Built Around the Lanai

If you look at Florida home design from above, the screened lanai isn't an add-on — it's the central organizing feature. Living rooms, kitchens, and master bedrooms all open onto the lanai through sliding glass doors. The indoor/outdoor flow is intentional. Our properties maintain this design philosophy, with large glass doors that open to create one continuous living space.

For vacation rentals specifically, the lanai solves a problem that northern homes don't have: how do you enjoy Florida's outdoor beauty without dealing with its insects, afternoon rain, and intense sun? The answer has been the same since the 1950s — put a screen around it. Simple, effective, and once you've experienced it, you'll wonder why every home in America doesn't have one.

Guest Tip: The lanai screen keeps out bugs but not rain blown sideways during thunderstorms. When afternoon storms hit (usually 3-5 PM in summer), close the lanai's roll-down storm shutters if available, or just move pool loungers away from the screen edges. Storms rarely last more than 30-45 minutes, and the post-storm cool-down is the best time to be outside.

Entertaining on the Lanai — What Groups Love

The lanai becomes the social hub for every group that stays with us. Families set up the kids in the pool while adults relax with drinks at the dining table. Bachelor/bachelorette parties use the space for pool games during the day and cocktail hour at night. Reunions spread across the loungers and dining areas with enough room for everyone without feeling packed in.

Grilling under screen is a game-changer. No fighting bugs while you flip burgers. No rain canceling your cookout. Our properties have gas grills (some with full outdoor kitchen setups) positioned on the lanai so you can cook, eat, and clean up without going inside. Several guests have told us it's the #1 feature they look for when booking — even above waterfront views.

Common Questions

Screened Lanai Questions

What is a screened lanai?

A screened lanai (pronounced luh-NYE) is an enclosed outdoor living space covered with fine mesh screening. In Florida, lanais typically surround the pool and patio area, creating a bug-free outdoor room. All Seascape Vacations properties feature screened lanais with pool access, seating areas, and outdoor dining space.

Do the screens actually keep out mosquitoes and no-see-ums?

Yes — our properties use 18x14 mesh fiberglass screening, which is fine enough to block both mosquitoes and no-see-ums (tiny biting sand flies that standard screens miss). We inspect and maintain screens between every guest stay to ensure there are no holes or gaps.

Can I use the lanai year-round?

Absolutely. Winter is peak lanai season with 70s temperatures and no bugs. Summer is warmer but the screen provides shade, and pool access keeps you cool. The lanai is usable 365 days a year — heated pools extend comfortable swimming from November through March.

Are the pools heated under the screened lanai?

Yes, all Seascape Vacations pools are heated and located within the screened lanai enclosure. Pool temperatures are maintained in the mid-80s°F year-round. Properties with hot tubs (The Oasis, Dockside Dreams, River House) also have those within the screen enclosure.

Is it safe for kids in the screened lanai area?

The screened enclosure adds an extra layer of safety by keeping the pool area enclosed. However, children should always be supervised around the pool. The screen doors have latches, and the enclosure prevents kids and pets from wandering toward the canal or street. Several of our properties have pool fences available as additional child safety features.
