Fourth of July Vacation Rentals — Florida Gulf Coast

Fireworks over the water, pool parties, and beach days. July 4th the way it should be.

Updated March 2026

By Seascape Vacations Team | Last Updated: March 2026

Fourth of July on Florida's Gulf Coast delivers everything the holiday promises — fireworks reflected on the water, pool parties that go all day, grilled burgers on the lanai, and kids who crash from exhaustion around 10 PM. Seascape Vacations' properties in Bradenton and Sarasota put you within striking distance of multiple fireworks shows, beautiful beaches, and the kind of vacation setup that makes your neighbors back home jealous of your Instagram feed.

Bradenton's July 4th scene works because of the water. The Manatee River, Sarasota Bay, and the Gulf itself create multiple vantage points for fireworks — and if you're staying at Dockside Dreams, our waterfront property, you might catch the show from the private dock or pool deck without fighting parking lots or crowds.

Where to Watch Fireworks Near Bradenton

Bradenton Riverwalk (10 min from our properties) hosts the city's biggest 4th of July celebration. Live music starts around 6 PM, food vendors line the waterfront path, and the fireworks launch over the Manatee River around 9:15 PM. The show lasts about 20 minutes, and the river reflections double every burst. Pro move: arrive by 5 PM to grab a spot near the amphitheater, or watch from the Green Bridge fishing pier for a less crowded angle.

Anna Maria Island (20 min) does a smaller but charming celebration — think small-town parade in the morning, beach picnics all afternoon, and fireworks off the Rod & Reel Pier at dusk. The island vibe is more laid-back and family-oriented, perfect if you want to avoid big-crowd energy. Holmes Beach and Bradenton Beach both have their own events with food trucks and live music on the sand.

Sarasota Bayfront (30 min) puts on a major show visible from Bayfront Park, Marina Jack, and St. Armands Circle. The show fires over Sarasota Bay, and the downtown restaurants and bars create a festive atmosphere. It's the biggest production in the area — more choreographed than Bradenton's show, with synchronized music on local radio. If you're staying at our Sarasota Luxe property, you're 15 minutes away.

The All-Day July 4th Itinerary (No Car Required After Arrival)

Here's how most of our guests spend the holiday — and why they end up booking the same week the following year. Morning: Hit the beach early. Anna Maria's Bean Point or Coquina Beach are both gorgeous and still relatively empty before 10 AM on July 4th. Bring the cooler, set up the umbrella, and spend 2-3 hours before the heat peaks. The Gulf water in early July averages 85°F — basically bathwater.

Midday: Back to the rental for the pool. This is where having your own place destroys hotel stays. The heated pool, the screened lanai keeping bugs out, the full kitchen for lunch prep — nobody's fighting over pool chairs or paying $14 for a hotel burger. Grill hot dogs on the gas grill, crack open the drinks, and let the kids wear themselves out in the pool. At The Oasis, the mini golf course and multiple hot tubs keep the adults and kids equally occupied.

Afternoon: Nap time for the little ones (and honestly, for the adults too). The bedrooms are air-conditioned to arctic levels, and the blackout curtains in most of our properties make 3 PM feel like midnight. Recharge for the evening.

Evening: Choose your fireworks adventure. Drive to the Riverwalk show, take the boat to AMI, or — the move that keeps getting repeated — stay at the property, fire up the grill for the real dinner (steaks, seafood, the works), and watch whatever distant fireworks you can see from the dock or pool deck. Multiple shows are visible from our Dockside Dreams and River House properties across the water. No parking, no traffic, no sunburnt crowds. Just your crew, the pool, and fireworks reflected on the canal.

Local Tip: Traffic on July 4th evening is brutal near Anna Maria Island — the bridge backs up for 60+ minutes after fireworks. If you go to AMI for the show, leave before it starts or plan to stay until 11 PM when traffic clears. Better yet, watch from the Bradenton side and skip the bridge entirely.

Booking the 4th — Timing and Availability

July 4th week books fast. Most of our properties are reserved 2-3 months ahead for the holiday week. We see two booking patterns: families who book a full Saturday-to-Saturday week encompassing the 4th, and smaller groups who want a long weekend (Thursday-Sunday or Friday-Monday). The full week gets a better nightly rate, but both options work.

Early July weather in Bradenton is peak summer: highs of 90-92°F, warm overnight lows around 75°F, and afternoon thunderstorms that blow through fast (usually 30-45 minutes) and actually cool things down. Mornings and evenings are gorgeous. The pool and Gulf water are both warm enough that you never hesitate to jump in.

Common Questions

Fourth of July Trip Planning

Where can I watch 4th of July fireworks near the rental?

Multiple options within 30 minutes: Bradenton Riverwalk (10 min, show at ~9:15 PM over the Manatee River), Anna Maria Island (20 min, beach fireworks off Rod & Reel Pier), and Sarasota Bayfront (30 min, major choreographed show). Dockside Dreams, our waterfront property, has fireworks views from the private dock.

Is the pool heated in July?

Yes, all Seascape pools are heated year-round to 82-86°F. In July, pool water is naturally warm (mid-80s) and doesn't need much heating. The Gulf of Mexico averages 85°F in early July — both pool and ocean are comfortable any time of day.

How far in advance should I book for July 4th?

July 4th week typically books 2-3 months ahead. We recommend reserving by April for the best property selection. Full-week bookings (Saturday to Saturday) get better nightly rates than short stays over just the holiday weekend.

What's the weather like for July 4th in Bradenton?

Peak summer: highs of 90-92°F, lows around 75°F, with afternoon thunderstorms that typically last 30-45 minutes and clear out. Mornings and evenings are beautiful. The heat is real but manageable with pool access, air conditioning, and Gulf breezes. Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen.

Can we grill out for July 4th at the rental?

Absolutely. Every Seascape property has a gas grill on the screened lanai. Several properties also have outdoor kitchen setups with counter space, sinks, and mini-fridges. The screened lanai keeps bugs away while you grill — a huge advantage over unscreened hotel patios in Florida summer.
