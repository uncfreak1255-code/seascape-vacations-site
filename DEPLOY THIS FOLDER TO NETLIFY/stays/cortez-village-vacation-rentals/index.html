Vacation Rentals Near Cortez Village, Florida

Florida's last working fishing village — fresh grouper, dolphin tours, and old-school coastal charm, all 5 minutes from your rental.

Updated March 2026

By Seascape Vacations Team | Last Updated: March 2026

Cortez is the real Florida, and it's disappearing fast. This tiny fishing village on the south bank of Sarasota Bay is one of the last working fishing communities on Florida's Gulf Coast. While the rest of the coastline has been developed into condos and resort hotels, Cortez still looks and functions like it did 50 years ago: commercial fishing boats tie up at the docks, nets dry in the sun, and the restaurants serve grouper that was swimming that morning. It's 5 minutes from Anna Maria Island but feels like a completely different world.

Seascape Vacations' Bradenton properties put you 5-8 minutes from Cortez — close enough for a casual lunch or sunset dinner, but in quieter residential neighborhoods with private pools and more space than the tiny waterfront lots in the village itself.

What Makes Cortez Special (And Why You Should Visit Before It Changes)

Cortez was settled in the 1880s by fishermen from North Carolina's Outer Banks. Their descendants still fish these waters — you can watch boats unload catches at the A.P. Bell Fish Company dock, one of the oldest continuous fish processing operations in Florida. The village is on the National Register of Historic Places, which has slowed (but not stopped) development pressure. Every year, a few more old fish houses get converted to boutique shops or restaurants. The character is still there, but the clock is ticking.

The FISH (Florida Institute for Saltwater Heritage) Preserve anchors the village with 95 acres of preserved coastal land, walking trails, and the annual Cortez Fishing Festival (third weekend in February). The preserve runs educational programs about commercial fishing history and Gulf ecology — it's genuinely interesting, not the kind of tourist-trap "educational experience" that makes you regret the detour.

Star Fish Company is the one you can't skip. The smoked fish dip is legendary — a smoky, creamy spread served with saltines that locals argue is the best on the Gulf Coast. Get there before noon on weekends to avoid the wait.

Activities From Cortez

Dolphin and Eco Tours: Several operators run tours from the Cortez marina. The boats head into Sarasota Bay where bottlenose dolphins are virtually guaranteed — the resident pods feed in the channel between Cortez and Longboat Key. Morning tours have the best dolphin activity; sunset tours add a spectacular sky show. Expect to pay $35-55/adult, with kids' discounts.

Fishing Charters: Cortez is a fishing village, so booking a charter here gets you a captain who actually grew up working these waters — not a transplant running a boat business. Half-day inshore trips ($400-600 for the boat) target redfish, snook, and trout in the flats and mangroves. Full-day offshore trips ($800-1,200) go for grouper, snapper, and kingfish in the Gulf. The commercial fishermen know spots that recreational captains don't.

The Cortez Bridge to AMI: The Cortez Bridge connects the village directly to Bradenton Beach on Anna Maria Island. It's a quick 5-minute drive across, which means staying near Cortez gives you both the authentic fishing village experience AND easy beach access. Bradenton Beach has rentals, restaurants, and less crowded sand than the north end of AMI.

Festival Alert: The Cortez Commercial Fishing Festival (third weekend in February) is the village's biggest event — fresh seafood, net-making demonstrations, live music, boat tours, and a crowd that's heavy on locals who actually know the village's history. It's free admission and one of the best cultural events on the Gulf Coast. Plan your vacation around it if possible.

Cortez vs. Tourist Florida — Why It Matters

Here's the thing about Cortez: it gives your vacation something that beach resorts and waterpark hotels can't. A sense of place. Real people doing real work on real boats, selling real fish to real restaurants. Your kids can watch a shrimper unload his catch at the dock and understand where food actually comes from. You can eat a grouper sandwich at Star Fish Company knowing the fish was caught that morning by the same family that runs the place. It's the kind of authentic experience that travel writers love to talk about but that most Florida vacations completely miss.

Staying at a Seascape rental gives you the best of both worlds: the comfort and space of a private vacation home (pool, kitchen, multiple bedrooms, screened lanai) with easy access to the kind of old Florida experience that's increasingly rare. Cortez for culture and food, AMI for beaches, and your rental for relaxation. That's a vacation with texture.

Common Questions

Cortez Village Questions

How far is Cortez Village from the vacation rentals?

Seascape Vacations' Bradenton properties are 5-8 minutes from Cortez Village by car. Dockside Dreams and River House are the closest. The Cortez Bridge also connects the village directly to Anna Maria Island (5 minutes), giving you easy access to both the village and the beaches.

What is Cortez Village known for?

Cortez is one of the last working fishing villages on Florida's Gulf Coast, settled in the 1880s. It's known for fresh-off-the-boat seafood restaurants (Star Fish Company is the standout), commercial fishing heritage, dolphin tours, fishing charters, and the annual Cortez Fishing Festival in February. The village is on the National Register of Historic Places.

Where should I eat in Cortez?

Star Fish Company is the must-visit — dockside seating, fish caught by their own boats, and the best smoked fish dip on the Gulf Coast ($12-22/plate). Swordfish Grill has a tiki bar atmosphere with blackened mahi tacos and waterfront views ($15-30). Both serve fresh Gulf seafood at reasonable prices compared to tourist-area restaurants.

Can I take a dolphin tour from Cortez?

Yes — several operators run dolphin and eco tours from the Cortez marina. Morning tours have the best dolphin activity in Sarasota Bay. Sunset tours combine dolphin spotting with spectacular sky views. Prices range from $35-55 per adult with children's discounts. Bottlenose dolphins are virtually guaranteed — resident pods feed in the channel daily.

When is the Cortez Fishing Festival?

The Cortez Commercial Fishing Festival is held the third weekend of February each year. It's free admission and features fresh seafood, net-making demonstrations, live music, boat tours, and local history. It's one of the best cultural events on the Gulf Coast and a great reason to plan a February vacation in the area.
