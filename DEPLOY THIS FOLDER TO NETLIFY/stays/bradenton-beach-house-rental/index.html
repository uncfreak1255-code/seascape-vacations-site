“Beach house” in Bradenton doesn’t mean a cramped cottage where you can hear your neighbor’s TV through the wall. You get a full-size home — private heated pool out back, maybe a dock on the canal, a game room for rainy afternoons, a hot tub for after the kids go to bed. The beach? It’s a quick drive across the Palma Sola Causeway to Anna Maria Island. Holmes Beach is the go-to for families. Coquina Beach has kayak rentals and a bayside cove. Manatee Public Beach has actual restrooms and a snack bar, which — if you’ve been to Florida beaches without those — you know matters more than it sounds.

Bradenton is also the gateway to some of the best inshore fishing on Florida’s Gulf Coast. Dockside Dreams has a 40-foot dock on a deep-water canal that runs straight to Tampa Bay — load up your gear, idle out past the manatee zone, and you’re fishing in 20 minutes. The village of Cortez, about 10 minutes south, is one of the last working fishing villages in Florida. Charter boats leave from there every morning. Fair warning on red tide: it can pop up August through October some years. Check the latest reports before booking summer dates. The fall mullet run, though? That’s when things get good — snook, redfish, and tarpon stacked up along the beaches and passes.

Price-wise, renting a beach house in Bradenton gets you 2–3x the space you’d get on Siesta Key or Anna Maria Island itself, at roughly half the nightly rate. Our homes have 4–5 bedrooms, private pools, and driveways that actually fit multiple cars. Robinson Preserve is 5 minutes away for morning paddleboard sessions. The Village of the Arts has restaurants and galleries that feel nothing like a tourist strip. You get the beach vacation without paying the “beachfront” tax.

Your Bradenton Beach Day, Mapped Out

Here’s a day that actually works (tested by our guests, refined over dozens of trips):

8:30 AM — Head to Manatee Public Beach on SR 64 (Manatee Avenue). Take it west across the Palma Sola Causeway, hang a left at the island. Free parking lot, actual restrooms, outdoor showers. Get there before 10 and you’ll snag a good spot without a fight.

11:30 AM — Walk or drive two minutes to the Sandbar Restaurant on Spring Avenue in Anna Maria City. Waterfront tables, your kids can play in the sand while you eat. The fish tacos are solid. Skip the wait by going right when they open for lunch.

1:00 PM — Head back to your rental. The drive takes 12–18 minutes depending on which property you’re in. Pool time. Nap time. Hot tub time. Whatever you need. This is the part where renting a beach house beats a hotel — you’ve got your own backyard.

5:30 PM — Two options. Drive back to Bean Point at the north tip of AMI for one of the best sunsets you’ll ever see (bring a chair and a drink). Or stay home — if you’re at River House, the sunset over the canal from the pool deck is hard to beat. Dockside Dreams has that waterfront view too.

7:30 PM — Dinner at Anna Maria Oyster Bar on Cortez Road (the mainland location, not the island one — shorter wait, same menu). The grouper sandwich is the move. Or fire up the grill at your rental — every property has one.

Bradenton Beach House Rental FAQs

What beach houses are available for rent near Bradenton?

Seascape Vacations manages 4 beach houses in the Bradenton area plus 1 in Sarasota. Dockside Dreams is a 4-bedroom waterfront home with a 40-ft boat dock, heated pool, and spa — sleeps 12. The Oasis is the biggest at 5 bedrooms, sleeping 16, with a massive pool, two hot tubs, mini golf, and a full game room. River House is a 4-bedroom canal-front retreat with a heated pool, spa, and sunset views over the water. Sarasota Luxe is a 4-bedroom home near St. Armands Circle and Lido Beach with a private pool. Every property has a private pool, and all are within 10–20 minutes of Gulf beaches.

How close are your beach houses to the actual beach?

Dockside Dreams is about 12 minutes from Holmes Beach on Anna Maria Island via Manatee Avenue (SR 64) over the Palma Sola Causeway. River House is roughly 15 minutes to Coquina Beach at AMI’s south end via Cortez Road. The Oasis is about 18 minutes to Manatee Public Beach. Sarasota Luxe is 10 minutes from Lido Beach and Siesta Key. You can also hit Palma Sola Causeway Beach on the way to the island — it’s a local favorite with calm water and free parking.

Do your Bradenton beach houses have pools?

Every single one. The Oasis has the biggest pool of the bunch plus 2 hot tubs — ideal for large groups or family reunions. Dockside Dreams has a heated pool and spa right next to the waterfront dock. River House has a heated pool and spa with canal sunset views from the pool deck. Sarasota Luxe has a private pool near St. Armands Circle. You won’t find a property in our lineup without one.

What’s the cheapest beach house rental near Bradenton?

River House and Bradenton Pool Home start around $250–$400/night depending on the season. Compare that to on-island Anna Maria Island rentals where you’d easily pay $500+ for less space, no pool, and street parking. When you book direct with Seascape Vacations, you save another 10–15% compared to Airbnb or VRBO because there are no platform service fees. A week-long stay can save you $300–$600 just by skipping the middleman.

Can I bring a boat to your Bradenton beach houses?

Absolutely. Dockside Dreams has a private 40-ft dock on a deep-water canal that connects directly to Tampa Bay and the Gulf of Mexico — perfect for fishing boats or pontoons up to about 35 feet. River House is also on a canal with water access. If you’re trailering a boat, the Cortez boat ramp is about 10 minutes from most of our properties, and it puts you right out into Sarasota Bay near the Intracoastal Waterway.

Is Bradenton better than Siesta Key for a beach house vacation?

Different vibes entirely. Siesta Key has world-famous quartz sand beaches, but the rentals are mostly smaller condos and cottages at premium prices. Bradenton gives you significantly larger homes — 4–5 bedrooms with private pools, hot tubs, docks — at roughly half the nightly rate. Anna Maria Island’s beaches are just 12–20 minutes from Bradenton, so you still get incredible Gulf access. Plus Bradenton has Robinson Preserve for kayaking and paddleboarding, the Village of the Arts for dinner, and way less tourist congestion. If you want space, value, and a more local feel — Bradenton wins.
