Not Your Typical Beach Rental

Most "beach house" searches in Bradenton return the same thing: cramped condos, dated cottages, or resort-adjacent apartments marketed as "beach houses" by property managers who've never set foot in them. That's not us.

Our beach houses are actual houses. Four and five bedrooms. Heated pools in every backyard. Kitchens with real cookware — not a single dented pot and a can opener. The kind of places where your whole family can eat dinner at the same table without someone sitting on a folding chair.

Each House Has a Personality

Dockside Dreams is the waterfront one. Private dock, heated pool and spa, and water views from the master bedroom. If you're bringing a boat or renting one, this is your spot — the dock can handle up to a 25-footer. Dolphins show up most mornings around 7:30. Not a guarantee, but close.

The Oasis is for the group that wants to be entertained. Five bedrooms, two hot tubs, a pool table, and — I'm not making this up — a private mini golf course in the backyard. Sleeps 16. It's the house that turns a family reunion from "obligation" to "when are we doing this again?"

River House sits on the Manatee River with sunset views that look photoshopped (they're not). Pool, spa, game room. The backyard faces west, so every evening is a show. Grab a chair, pour something cold, and watch the sky turn orange.

Sarasota Luxe is the culture play. Located in downtown Sarasota, you're walking distance to St. Armands Circle, 10 minutes from Siesta Key, and 5 minutes from the Ringling Museum. Private pool, sleek finishes, and the best restaurant scene on the Gulf Coast right outside your door.

The Beach Situation

Your nearest Gulf beach from our Bradenton houses is Coquina Beach on the south end of Anna Maria Island — about 12 minutes over the Cortez Bridge. It's got the largest parking lot on AMI, which matters more than you'd think. Manatee Public Beach in Holmes Beach is 15 minutes via Manatee Avenue. Both have restrooms, showers, and are family-friendly with shallow Gulf water.

For a change of pace, Longboat Key beaches are 20 minutes south — less crowded, more upscale vibe. And Siesta Key Beach, consistently rated the #1 beach in America, is about 35 minutes. The sand there is 99% quartz crystal — it literally doesn't get hot. Worth the drive at least once.
