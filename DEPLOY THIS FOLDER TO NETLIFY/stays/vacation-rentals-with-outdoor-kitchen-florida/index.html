Florida Vacation Rentals with Outdoor Kitchens

Cook under the stars (but above the bugs). Full grilling setups, poolside dining, and screened-in comfort.

Updated March 2026

By Seascape Vacations Team | Last Updated: March 2026

The outdoor kitchen changes how you vacation. Instead of piling into a restaurant every night (at $40-60 per person), you fire up the grill on the screened lanai, throw on whatever you grabbed from Publix or the fresh seafood market, and eat by the pool while the kids swim. It's the vacation meal experience restaurants can't match — barefoot, no wait, no tipping on a table of 12.

Every Seascape Vacations property has outdoor grilling and dining setups within the screened lanai area. Some have full outdoor kitchen stations with counter space, sinks, and prep areas. Others have premium gas grills with adjacent dining tables. All of them beat the indoor kitchen for group meals during a Florida vacation, because once you eat outside with the pool lit up at night, going back inside feels wrong.

Where to Source Ingredients (Skip the Tourist Traps)

The Bradenton Farmers' Market at Riverwalk runs on Saturdays from October through May — outstanding local produce, baked goods, and prepared foods. For seafood, Star Fish Company in Cortez Village sells fresh-off-the-boat grouper, snapper, shrimp, and stone crab claws when in season. Captain Brian's at the Cortez docks is another local favorite for same-day catches.

Publix (the Florida grocery chain) has excellent meat and seafood departments. The Publix on Cortez Road is 5 minutes from most of our properties. For a more upscale option, Detwiler's Farm Market on 14th Street has an insane selection of fresh produce, meats, prepared foods, and local honey. Their smoked fish dip is absurdly good — grab a container, some crackers, and eat it on the lanai as an appetizer.

Meal Ideas That Work on Vacation (Keep It Simple)

Nobody wants to spend their vacation doing elaborate meal prep. The best outdoor kitchen meals are simple, shareable, and take under 30 minutes. Here's what our guests end up doing most often — and what they rave about:

Grouper tacos from fresh catch: Pick up grouper at Star Fish Company, season with Old Bay and lime, grill 4 minutes per side, stuff into tortillas with slaw and mango salsa. Total time: 20 minutes. Total cost for 8 people: maybe $50. Restaurant equivalent: $200+.

Shrimp boil on the grill: Publix sells pre-seasoned shrimp boil kits. Throw everything in foil packets on the grill. Corn, potatoes, sausage, shrimp — done in 25 minutes. Spread newspaper on the outdoor table, dump everything out, eat with your hands. This is peak Florida vacation energy.

Steak night with a view: Costco trip for steaks (the Costco on University Parkway in Sarasota is 20 minutes). Season simply, grill to preference, pair with Publix potato salad and a good bottle of wine. Eating a perfectly grilled steak by your private pool at sunset is a vacation moment that sticks with people.

Grilling Tip: Florida's humidity affects grill performance differently than up north. Preheat the grill 10-15 minutes with the lid closed to burn off moisture and get proper searing temperatures. We keep propane tanks full between guests, but for longer stays, replacement tanks are available at the Publix and Home Depot on Cortez Road.

The Cost Savings Are Real (Do the Math)

A family of 8 eating out for dinner in Bradenton averages $45-65 per person including tax, tip, and drinks. That's $360-520 per dinner. Over a 7-night stay, restaurant dinners alone cost $2,500-3,600. Cook 4 of those 7 dinners on the outdoor kitchen instead — groceries for 4 grill dinners for 8 people runs about $300-400 total. That's $1,500-2,500 saved on food alone. Factor in the 10-15% you save booking direct with us, and the total savings pay for extra activities, a fishing charter, or a nice bottle of something for that final night dinner.

Common Questions

Outdoor Kitchen Questions

What type of grill is available?

All Seascape Vacations properties have gas grills maintained with full propane tanks. The Oasis has a large multi-burner gas grill with prep counter space. Other properties have quality 3-4 burner gas grills with side prep tables. Grilling utensils, spatulas, and tongs are provided at each property.

Is the outdoor kitchen area screened in?

Yes, all grilling and outdoor dining areas are within the screened lanai enclosure. You can cook and eat outside without dealing with mosquitoes, no-see-ums, or other Florida insects. The screens also keep out debris and provide shade during afternoon hours.

What cooking supplies are provided?

Each property comes with a fully equipped indoor kitchen (pots, pans, plates, utensils, cutting boards, knives) plus outdoor grilling tools. Indoor kitchens have standard appliances — oven, microwave, dishwasher, refrigerator, coffee maker. You'll just need to bring or buy your own food and spices.

Where's the nearest grocery store?

Publix on Cortez Road is 5 minutes from most properties. Detwiler's Farm Market on 14th Street has exceptional produce and prepared foods. For fresh seafood, Star Fish Company in Cortez Village sells straight off the boat. Costco on University Parkway in Sarasota is 20 minutes away for bulk shopping.

How many people can eat outdoors?

Outdoor dining capacity ranges from 8 seats at our smaller properties to 16 at The Oasis. All dining areas are under the screened lanai with comfortable chairs and weather-resistant tables. For larger groups at The Oasis, the two separate dining areas can accommodate the whole party comfortably.
