Try searching for "pet-friendly vacation rentals on Anna Maria Island" and you'll see the issue fast. Most on-island properties don't allow dogs at all. The ones that do? They're typically small cottages with shared yards, strict weight limits (under 25 lbs — so your Lab doesn't qualify), and pet fees that rival a night's stay.

Dog-Friendly Beaches and Parks

Anna Maria Island allows dogs on beaches before 9 AM and after 5 PM year-round — leashed only. The best access point for dogs is Bayfront Park in Anna Maria City (north end). It's less crowded than Manatee Public Beach, and the bayfront side has calmer water that most dogs prefer over Gulf waves.

For off-leash time, head to Bayshore Gardens Dog Park in Bradenton — about 15 minutes from our properties. It's got separate areas for large and small dogs, water stations, and shade structures. GT Bray Park also has a dog-friendly area with a walking trail.

Robinson Preserve is a hidden gem for dog owners. The 600-acre nature preserve has miles of walking trails through mangroves and salt marshes. Dogs must be leashed, but the trails are wide and shaded, and most mornings you'll see more herons than people. Bring water — there aren't fountains on the outer trails.

Pet-Friendly Dining Near AMI

Florida's restaurant scene is surprisingly dog-friendly. On Anna Maria Island, The Sandbar has a patio that welcomes dogs — and the sunset views from there are ridiculous. Duffy's Tavern in Holmes Beach is casual and dog-friendly. The Waterfront Restaurant has outdoor seating where well-behaved dogs are welcome.

Off-island, Motorworks Brewing in downtown Bradenton has one of the largest dog-friendly patios in the area — plus solid craft beer and food trucks on weekends. Ortygia in downtown Bradenton does upscale Italian with a dog-friendly courtyard. And if you're heading to Sarasota, Owen's Fish Camp on Burns Lane is a must — dogs welcome on the porch, and the she-crab soup is the best on the Gulf Coast.
