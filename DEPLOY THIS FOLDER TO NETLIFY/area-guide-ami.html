Why Guests Love Anna Maria Island

Anna Maria Island is a 7-mile barrier island on Florida's Gulf Coast, known for its relaxed atmosphere, impressive white-sand beaches, and authentic Old Florida character. Unlike more developed coastal areas, AMI has preserved its small-town charm with local boutiques, waterfront restaurants, and no high-rise buildings.

The island is divided into three distinct communities: Anna Maria City at the north end (historic, quiet), Holmes Beach in the middle (family-friendly, most amenities), and Bradenton Beach at the south (affordable, beach bars). Each offers a unique vacation experience.

With warm Gulf waters, incredible fishing, and some of the most beautiful sunsets in Florida, Anna Maria Island consistently ranks among the state's top beach destinations.

Island Highlights

What Makes AMI Special

🏖️

Pristine Beaches

Powdery white sand and calm, warm Gulf waters. Bean Point, Manatee Beach, and Coquina Beach offer diverse experiences.

🌅

Eye-catching Sunsets

The west-facing Gulf provides spectacular sunsets every evening. The City Pier is perfect for sunset viewing.

🏡

Old Florida Charm

No high-rises, no chain restaurants. Just local shops, family-owned eateries, and genuine small-town hospitality.

🐬

Wildlife Encounters

Dolphins, manatees, sea turtles, and diverse bird life. Take a nature tour or spot wildlife from your rental's dock.

🎣

Outstanding Fishing

Inshore flats, nearshore reefs, and offshore deep sea fishing. Charter boats depart daily from local marinas.

🚴

Easy to Explore

Bike-friendly island with trolley service. Everything you need is within a short ride from your vacation rental.

Find Your Perfect Location

Each area of Anna Maria Island offers a distinct experience.

Anna Maria City

The historic north end. Quiet, residential, with Bean Point Beach and the iconic City Pier. Perfect for couples and those seeking tranquility.

🏖️ Bean Point 🍽️ Rod & Reel Pier 💰 Premium

Holmes Beach

The island's center and most populated area. Best for families with grocery stores, restaurants, and easy beach access at Manatee Beach.

Bradenton Beach

The lively south end. Famous beach bars, more affordable rentals, and the memorable Coquina Beach. Great for groups and nightlife.
