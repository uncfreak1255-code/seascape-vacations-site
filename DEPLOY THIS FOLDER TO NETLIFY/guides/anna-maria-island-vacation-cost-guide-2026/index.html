Anna Maria Island Vacation Cost Guide 2026

Updated March 2026 · Real pricing from local property managers · 8 min read

The Quick Numbers A week on Anna Maria Island runs $3,500–$7,500 for a family of four in 2026. Vacation rentals average $275–$400/night for a 3-bedroom home. Book directly from property managers (not Airbnb or VRBO) to save 15–35% on rental costs alone. Peak season is February through April — visit in May or September for the best value without sacrificing weather.

Every year, the same question lands in our inbox around January: "How much should we budget for Anna Maria Island?"

Fair question. And the internet is shockingly bad at answering it. Most "cost guides" are either outdated, based on hotel pricing (there are barely any hotels on AMI), or written by someone who's never actually been here. So here's the real breakdown from people who manage vacation rentals on the island and see the actual numbers every week.

Rentals: Where the Real Savings Are

Your accommodation is 50–60% of the total trip cost. So this is where smart decisions make the biggest difference.

AMI Rental Pricing by Property Type

The island has three distinct towns — Anna Maria (north end, quieter), Holmes Beach (center, most restaurants), and Bradenton Beach (south end, closest to mainland). Pricing varies:

  • 1-bedroom cottage, Holmes Beach: $175–$250/night — perfect for couples, usually a short walk to the beach
  • 2-bedroom condo, Bradenton Beach: $200–$325/night — good for small families, some have pool access
  • 3-bedroom home, any town: $275–$450/night — the sweet spot for families, usually with a full kitchen and outdoor space
  • 4+ bedroom waterfront with pool & dock: $400–$650/night — the full experience, great for multi-family trips where you split costs

Properties in the Bradenton mainland area (just across the bridge) run 20–30% less than equivalent properties on the island itself — and you're only a 10-minute drive to the beach. Something worth considering if you're budget-conscious. Our Bradenton waterfront homes start around $250/night with pools, docks, and way more space than a cramped island condo.

Dining: From Fish Tacos to White Tablecloth

Food on AMI ranges from casual and affordable to special-occasion pricing. Here's what actual meals cost:

Casual dining (most meals): Expect $15–$25 per person. The Sandbar on Anna Maria is our go-to — grab a table on the beach and watch the sunset over grouper sandwiches. Skinny's Place in Holmes Beach has the best burgers on the island for under $14. And don't skip the waterfront restaurants in Bradenton Beach — the Rod & Reel Pier restaurant is an island institution.

Mid-range restaurants: $30–$50 per person. Beach Bistro for a splurge dinner, or Waterfront Restaurant for solid seafood with a view.

The smart move: Get a rental with a full kitchen and cook breakfast and lunch at home. The Publix in Holmes Beach or the Walmart Neighborhood Market on Cortez Road have everything you need. We tell guests: cook two meals, eat one out. That formula keeps a family of four's food budget around $100–$140/day while still enjoying the island's restaurants.

Activities: What's Free, What's Not

One of the best things about AMI — a lot of the best stuff costs nothing.

Free Activities

Coquina Beach (south end, great shells and calmer water for kids). Manatee Beach Park in Holmes Beach — showers, picnic tables, and a playground. Walking or biking Pine Avenue in Anna Maria City for the shops and galleries. The Rod & Reel Pier (free to walk, $5 to fish). Sunset at Bean Point — the north tip of the island where Tampa Bay meets the Gulf. Kayaking from your rental dock if you've got a waterfront property (bring your own or rent for ~$45/day).

Getting Here & Getting Around

Nearest airports: Sarasota-Bradenton International (SRQ) is 25 minutes from the island — small airport, easy in and out. Tampa International (TPA) is about 75 minutes but usually has cheaper flights and more options.

You'll want a car. There's a free trolley that runs the length of the island, but it's slow, crowded in season, and doesn't help for grocery runs or off-island adventures. Rental cars from SRQ run $40–$70/day; from TPA, $35–$60/day. Gas is around $3.20/gallon on the island (slightly cheaper at the Wawa on Cortez Road).

Parking: free at most beaches except during peak season when the lots at Manatee Beach and Coquina fill up by 10am. Another argument for staying at a rental in Bradenton — you'll always have parking and just drive over to the beach.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a week on Anna Maria Island cost in 2026?

A week-long Anna Maria Island vacation for a family of four typically costs $3,500–$7,500 total in 2026. That breaks down to roughly $1,200–$4,500 for the rental, $600–$1,200 for food, $300–$800 for activities, and $200–$500 for transportation. The biggest variable is your accommodation — booking direct from a local property manager saves 15–35% versus OTA platforms like Airbnb.

When is the cheapest time to visit Anna Maria Island?

September and October offer the lowest rates — 30–40% below peak season. A 3-bedroom waterfront home that runs $450/night in March might be $250–$300 in early fall. May is the hidden gem: water's warm, crowds are gone, and prices haven't dropped to hurricane-season lows yet (because weather is still excellent).

Is Anna Maria Island expensive compared to other Florida beaches?

It's mid-to-upper range for the Gulf Coast. Cheaper than Naples, Sanibel, or Longboat Key. Roughly on par with Siesta Key. More expensive than Panama City Beach, Treasure Island, or Indian Rocks Beach. The premium buys you a no-high-rise island with genuine Old Florida character — which, depending on what you value, might be worth every penny.

How much do vacation rentals cost per night on AMI?

In 2026: $175/night for a basic 1-bedroom cottage up to $650+/night for a large waterfront home with pool and dock. The average 3-bedroom home falls in the $275–$400/night range. Holmes Beach properties tend to be slightly cheaper than Anna Maria city or Bradenton Beach. Properties on the Bradenton mainland, just across the bridge, run 20–30% less for equivalent size and amenities.

Can you do Anna Maria Island on a budget?

Yes. Visit in shoulder season (May or September–October), book your rental directly from the management company, cook breakfast and lunch at the rental, and lean into free activities: Coquina Beach, Rod & Reel Pier, Bean Point sunsets, Pine Avenue browsing, and Robinson Preserve. A budget-conscious family of four can absolutely do a week for under $3,000 — we've seen guests do it happily.
