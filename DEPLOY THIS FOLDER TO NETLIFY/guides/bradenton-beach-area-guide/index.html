The Lively South End of AMI

Positioned at the scenic southern tip of Anna Maria Island, Bradenton Beach offers a distinctly lively and walkable vacation experience. Here, the island narrows, meaning that many vacation rentals offer glimpses of both the sparkling Gulf of Mexico to the west and the tranquil Intracoastal Waterway to the east.

The crown jewel of this community is Historic Bridge Street. It serves as a lively hub where visitors can easily stroll between eclectic boutiques, lively waterfront restaurants, and the iconic fishing pier. It's one of the few places on the island with a dedicated nightlife scene, making it popular for those looking for live music after sunset.

Nature lovers will adore Coquina Beach, which stretches down the southern coast. Famous for its sprawling, shaded picnic areas and pristine sands, it's a brilliant place to spend the day searching for shells, swimming, or riding a bike down the paved, multi-use trail.

Bradenton Beach FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Bradenton Beach located?

Bradenton Beach is located at the southern tip of Anna Maria Island, making it the closest island community to Longboat Key and the Cortez Bridge to the mainland.

What is Historic Bridge Street?

Historic Bridge Street is the commercial center of Bradenton Beach. Once the site of the first bridge to the island, it is now a highly walkable district filled with boutiques, art galleries, restaurants, mini-golf, and a lively fishing pier.

Is Coquina Beach nice?

Coquina Beach, stretching along the southern end of Bradenton Beach, is renowned for its vast expanse of white sand, ample free parking, picnic pavilions shaded by Australian pines, and paved multi-use trail. It's fantastic for families and beach strolls.

Does Bradenton Beach have good nightlife?

Yes, compared to the rest of Anna Maria Island, Bradenton Beach has a more energetic nightlife scene. The Bridge Street area features several venues with live music, casual bars, and waterfront dining.
