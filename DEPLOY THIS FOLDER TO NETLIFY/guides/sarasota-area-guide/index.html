Why Choose Sarasota?

Sarasota is Florida's Cultural Coast — a sophisticated destination where first-rate arts, fine dining, and gorgeous beaches converge. From the Ringling Museum to the Van Wezel Performing Arts Hall, culture is woven into the fabric of this beautiful city.

Stay in Sarasota and you're perfectly positioned to explore Siesta Key (ranked #1 beach in the US), stroll the upscale shops of St. Armands Circle, and enjoy some of Florida's best restaurants — all within minutes of your vacation rental.

Our Sarasota properties offer luxury accommodations with easy access to both the colorful downtown scene and the peaceful barrier island beaches.

Sarasota Beyond the Guidebook

The tourist websites tell you about Siesta Key Beach and Main Street. That's a fine start, but it's not the real Sarasota.

The real Sarasota is knowing that Dry Dock Waterbar on the bay side serves better fish than the overpriced beachfront spots. It's timing your beach walk for early morning before the crowds, then heading inland to breakfast where the grouper hash is legendary. Our guests who rent Sarasota Luxe learn fast: spend your afternoon south of Siesta Key at Casey Key, where the crowds thin and the beach gets quieter. Catch sunset from the Ringling Museum grounds instead of fighting for space at Siesta Beach. That's Sarasota living — and it's what separates a generic vacation from a trip that changes how you see Florida.

One more thing: if you're visiting between November and April, the Sarasota Farmers Market on Saturday mornings at Lemon Avenue is worth the early wake-up. Local honey, Gulf shrimp, and produce from Myakka farms. Skip the overpriced grocery stores.
