Restaurant Guide

Beach House Waterfront Restaurant

Updated March 2026 • 6 min read

Beach House Waterfront Restaurant is one of Anna Maria Island's most iconic dining destinations — and for good reason. Perched directly on the sand at 200 Gulf Drive North, Bradenton Beach, FL 34217, it's one of the only restaurants in the area where you can literally have your feet in the sand while watching dolphins play in the Gulf.

Why Beach House Is a Must-Visit

Beach House Waterfront Restaurant sits right on the beach in Bradenton Beach, offering unobstructed views of the Gulf of Mexico. Unlike many "waterfront" restaurants that are technically near the water, Beach House delivers true beachfront dining — tables on the sand, waves crashing just feet away, and sunsets that will make you forget about everything else.

The restaurant has been a staple of the Anna Maria Island dining scene for years, drawing both locals and visitors. It's the kind of place where you walk in wearing flip-flops and a cover-up and feel perfectly at home.

What to Order

The menu leans heavily into fresh Gulf seafood, which is exactly what you want when you're sitting on the beach. Standout dishes include the grouper sandwich (a local obsession), the coconut shrimp, and the fresh catch of the day prepared grilled, blackened, or fried. Their fish tacos are consistently excellent.

For drinks, the frozen cocktails are the way to go — the Rum Runner and Piña Colada are made strong and cold, perfect for a 90°F afternoon. Happy hour runs daily and makes an already-reasonable menu even more affordable.

Hours, Parking & Practical Tips

  • Hours: Open daily for lunch and dinner, typically 11:30 AM – 9:00 PM (call ahead for seasonal hours)
  • Parking: The restaurant has a small lot, but it fills up fast. Metered street parking on Gulf Drive is your best bet. Arrive before 5 PM for sunset seating without a long wait.
  • Reservations: They don't take reservations for outdoor seating — it's first-come, first-served on the beach. Indoor seating can sometimes be reserved for larger parties.
  • Kid-friendly: Very. The beach setting keeps kids entertained, and there's a children's menu.
  • Dog-friendly: Dogs are welcome on the outdoor patio area.

Insider Tips from Locals

Go for a late lunch (around 3 PM) on a weekday. You'll get beachfront seating without a wait, the afternoon light is gorgeous for photos, and you can stay right through sunset. Order the grouper — if it's fresh, it's the best on the island.
