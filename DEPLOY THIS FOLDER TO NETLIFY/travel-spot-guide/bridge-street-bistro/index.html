Bridge Street Bistro

Updated March 2026 • 5 min read

Bridge Street Bistro is one of those places that surprises first-time visitors. Located at 111 Gulf Drive South, Bradenton Beach, FL 34217, right at the heart of the historic Bridge Street district, this intimate bistro brings European-inspired cuisine to a laid-back beach town — and it works beautifully.

Why Bridge Street Bistro Stands Out

In a dining scene dominated by casual seafood shacks (which are great — don't get us wrong), Bridge Street Bistro offers something different: thoughtful, chef-driven cuisine in a warm, intimate setting. The restaurant is small, the atmosphere is romantic, and the menu pulls from French and Mediterranean influences while staying rooted in fresh Florida ingredients.

It's situated in the walkable Bridge Street district, which is Bradenton Beach's charming "village" area with local shops, galleries, and the fishing pier. Dinner here pairs perfectly with an evening stroll through the neighborhood.

What to Order

The menu rotates seasonally, but signature dishes include pan-seared duck breast, fresh Gulf fish with beurre blanc, and hand-cut pasta. The charcuterie board is an excellent start, and the crème brûlée is a must for dessert.

The craft cocktail program is a cut above what you'll find at most AMI restaurants — well-balanced classics and creative seasonal specials. The wine list is thoughtfully curated with selections that pair well with the seafood-forward menu.

Hours, Parking & Practical Tips

  • Hours: Dinner service nightly, typically from 5:00 PM. Check for seasonal lunch hours.
  • Parking: Street parking along Bridge Street and Gulf Drive. The public lot at the end of Bridge Street is free.
  • Reservations: Strongly recommended — the restaurant is small and fills up, especially on weekends.
  • Dress code: Smart casual. It's still a beach town, but this is a step up from flip-flops.
  • Price range: $$$ — expect $30-$45 for entrées.

Insider Tips from Locals

Book a table for an early dinner (5:30 PM), then walk down Bridge Street to the fishing pier for sunset. The Bistro is also one of the best date-night spots on the island — intimate, quiet, and the food actually lives up to the setting.
