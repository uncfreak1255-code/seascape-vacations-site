Mar Vista Dockside Restaurant & Pub

Updated March 2026 • 6 min read

Mar Vista Dockside Restaurant & Pub is the hidden gem that locals don't really want tourists to know about — but we're telling you anyway. Tucked away at 760 Broadway Street, Longboat Key, FL 34228, this no-frills waterfront pub sits on Sarasota Bay with a vibe that screams "Old Florida."

Why Mar Vista Is Special

Mar Vista is the antithesis of a fancy waterfront restaurant. There's no valet, no dress code, and the décor hasn't been updated in decades — and that's exactly why people love it. You're here for the waterfront setting on Sarasota Bay, the massive live oaks draped in string lights, and some of the best casual seafood on the Gulf Coast.

What makes Mar Vista truly unique: you can arrive by boat. The restaurant has its own dock, and on any given evening you'll see everything from kayaks to center consoles to sailboats tied up out back. It's one of the few genuine "dock and dine" spots in the area.

What to Order

Start with the smoked fish dip — it's house-smoked and widely considered the best on the island. The fish & chips are a reliable classic, and the blackened mahi sandwich is excellent. For something heartier, the coconut shrimp basket and crab cakes are both crowd favorites.

Beer drinkers will appreciate the solid craft selection, and the frozen drinks are appropriately tropical. Happy hour is one of the best deals in the area — come early and park yourself under the oaks.

Hours, Parking & Practical Tips

  • Hours: Open daily, typically 11:00 AM – 9:00 PM (seasonal variations apply)
  • Parking: Small gravel lot — it fills up quick on weekends. Come by bike, boat, or early.
  • By boat: Free dock space for diners, first-come first-served. Approach from Sarasota Bay.
  • Reservations: Not typically accepted — first-come, first-served seating.
  • Kid-friendly: Yes, very casual and relaxed.
  • Best time to visit: Weekday afternoons for guaranteed outdoor seating.

Insider Tips from Locals

Go at sunset on a weeknight. Grab the smoked fish dip and a cold beer at one of the outdoor tables under the oak tree. Watch the pelicans dive-bomb for mullet while the sun drops behind the mangroves. This is Florida at its finest — no pretense, just great food and water views.
