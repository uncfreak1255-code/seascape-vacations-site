Restaurant Guide

The Sandbar Restaurant

Updated March 2026 • 6 min read

The Sandbar Restaurant is Anna Maria Island's premier fine-dining waterfront experience. Located at 100 Spring Avenue, Anna Maria, FL 34216, right on the beach at the north end of the island, The Sandbar offers the kind of sunset dinner that makes a vacation unforgettable.

Why The Sandbar Is Worth the Splurge

While most AMI restaurants lean casual, The Sandbar elevates the experience without being stuffy. Floor-to-ceiling windows frame the Gulf of Mexico, the wine list is curated and extensive, and the kitchen focuses on locally-sourced seafood prepared with real culinary technique. This is where you go for an anniversary dinner, a special celebration, or just when you want something beyond grouper sandwiches.

The setting is stunning — you're looking directly at the Gulf, and the sunset views from the dining room are among the best on the island. They also have a beachside patio for a more relaxed experience.

What to Order

The menu changes seasonally, but look for the pan-seared grouper (prepared with more finesse than you'll find anywhere else on AMI), the diver scallops, and the seafood tower for a showstopper starter. The filet mignon is also excellent for non-seafood diners.

Sunday brunch at The Sandbar has become an institution — think Gulf views with eggs Benedict, mimosas, and live steel drum music. It books up, so call ahead.

Hours, Parking & Practical Tips

  • Hours: Dinner nightly from 5:00 PM. Sunday brunch typically 10:00 AM – 2:00 PM. Closed for lunch on weekdays.
  • Parking: Small lot fills up fast. Street parking available on Spring Avenue and nearby residential streets.
  • Reservations: Highly recommended, especially for Friday/Saturday dinner and Sunday brunch. Book at least a few days ahead during peak season (January-April).
  • Dress code: Smart casual — sundresses and collared shirts fit right in. No swimsuits.
  • Price range: $$$ — entrées typically $28-$48.

Insider Tips from Locals

Request a window table when you make your reservation — specifically, ask for the west-facing side. Time your reservation for 30-45 minutes before sunset. The view alone is worth it. And don't skip the key lime pie — it's the best on the island.

Where to Stay Nearby
