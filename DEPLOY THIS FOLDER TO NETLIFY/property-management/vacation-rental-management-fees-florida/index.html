What You're Paying For

What's Actually Included in a Management Fee?

A "management fee" is a catch-all term. Two companies quoting 25% can offer wildly different scopes of work. Before you compare percentages, make sure you're comparing the same services.

Usually Included

  • ✓ Airbnb, VRBO, Booking.com listing management
  • ✓ Dynamic pricing adjustments
  • ✓ Guest communication (pre-booking through checkout)
  • ✓ Cleaning coordination
  • ✓ Lock/access management
  • ✓ Monthly owner statements
  • ✓ State & local tax remittance
  • ✓ Basic maintenance coordination

Often Extra (Ask First)

  • ⚠ Professional photography
  • ⚠ Direct booking website
  • ⚠ Owner supplies/restocking
  • ⚠ Deep cleaning fees
  • ⚠ Maintenance callouts over $X
  • ⚠ Damage claim processing
  • ⚠ Annual property inspections
  • ⚠ Revenue consulting reports

At Seascape, our management fee covers everything on the left — plus professional photography for new properties and a direct booking website. We don't nickel-and-dime on maintenance coordination for standard issues. The only additional costs are actual cleaning fees (billed to guests via the booking platform) and significant maintenance jobs that require outside contractors.

Watch Out

Hidden Fees That Can Inflate Your Real Cost by 8–12%

The headline percentage rarely tells the whole story. The companies that advertise the lowest rates often make it up elsewhere. Here's what to look for when reading the fine print:

Fee Type Typical Cost Watch For
Setup / onboarding fee $200–$500 One-time, but often waived — just ask
Professional photography $300–$600 Non-negotiable — bad photos cost more than the fee
Credit card / payment processing 2–3% of gross Adds up fast on a $50K/year property
Maintenance callout fee $50–$150 per call On top of the actual repair cost
Damage claim processing $50–$100 per claim Even when it's the guest's fault
Deep cleaning (beyond turnover) $150–$400 Ask what triggers a "deep clean" vs. standard
Early termination penalty 1–3 months revenue Most red flag of all — avoid any contract with these
Contractor markup warning: Some management companies mark up maintenance invoices by 15–25% on top of what contractors actually charge. Always ask: "Do you pass through contractor invoices at cost, or do you add a markup?"

Local Market

Management Fees on Anna Maria Island and in Bradenton

The Bradenton–Anna Maria Island corridor is a strong vacation rental market. You've got 1,600+ active short-term rentals, March and February as the two busiest booking months, and guests willing to book 86 days in advance for peak-season stays. The market rewards well-managed properties — and punishes poorly presented ones, because competition is real.

On Anna Maria Island specifically, nightly rates for a well-positioned 4-bedroom run $350–$600+. At 65% occupancy over 52 weeks, that's $65,000–$80,000 in gross annual revenue for a top performer. A 25% management fee costs $16,000–$20,000 per year — but if the manager's pricing and occupancy optimization adds 20% over what you'd get self-managing, the fee pays for itself and then some.

In Bradenton proper (not AMI), rates are lower — typically $200–$350/night for a 3–4 bedroom with a pool. The average across all listing types sits around $301/night ADR. That means a typical fully-managed Bradenton property grosses $40,000–$55,000 per year, with management fees running $9,000–$15,000 annually at 22–28%.

The Math

Is Hiring a Manager Worth It? Running the Numbers

The honest answer: it depends heavily on how you value your time and how far you live from your property. But the financial case is stronger than most self-managing owners expect — because the comparison isn't "my revenue minus the fee" vs. "my revenue with no fee." It's "managed revenue minus fee" vs. "self-managed revenue with no fee."

Self-managing owners typically leave money on the table in three ways: pricing that's too static (not adjusting for Sarasota Music Festival, spring break, or late-season deals); listings that look fine but aren't optimized (slightly worse photos, no dynamic description updates); and vacancy gaps around owner stays or "soft" months where they don't push promotions. Add up the cost of those missed opportunities across a year and it frequently exceeds 15–20% of gross revenue — meaning management pays for itself on revenue lift alone.

For owners outside Bradenton or out of state, professional management isn't just financially justified; it's operationally necessary for a well-run property.

Evaluation Framework

How to Compare Vacation Rental Management Companies

Don't shop on percentage alone — that's how owners end up paying the least and getting the worst results. Here's the five-question framework we'd use if we were evaluating ourselves against a competitor:

1. Ask for a complete fee schedule in writing

The headline % and the real cost are often different things. Get a written list of every fee — setup, photography, maintenance coordination, cleaning, processing. If they hesitate, that's your answer.

2. Look up their current listings on Airbnb and VRBO

Search for their properties on the platforms. Are the photos professional? Are descriptions compelling and specific? Do the listings have strong reviews? This is the fastest way to evaluate marketing quality — no sales pitch needed.

3. Ask specifically: local team or remote?

National companies often handle guest issues from a call center in another state. When a guest calls at 11pm because the AC stopped working, is someone actually driving over? In the Gulf Coast summer heat, that matters — a lot.

4. Read owner reviews, not guest reviews

Guest reviews tell you about the property experience. Owner reviews (on Google, not just their website) tell you about the management relationship — communication quality, transparency on revenue, how they handle problems. Find them on Google Maps for the management company, not Airbnb.

5. Check the contract for exit clauses

Any contract with a 6–12 month penalty for leaving is a red flag. Good management companies earn their owners' business every month. We offer flexible terms because we're confident in the results — you shouldn't be locked in if we're not delivering.

Frequently Asked Questions

Management Fees — FAQ

How much do vacation rental managers charge in Florida? +
Vacation rental management fees in Florida typically range from 20% to 35% of gross rental revenue for full-service management. National chains like Vacasa charge 25–35%, while local boutique managers on the Gulf Coast generally charge 20–28%. Some partial-service platforms (like Evolve) charge as low as 10%, but those require owners to handle guest communication and local coordination. In the Bradenton and Anna Maria Island market specifically, full-service fees average 22–28%.
What is included in a vacation rental management fee? +
A full-service management fee typically includes listing creation and optimization on Airbnb, VRBO, and Booking.com; dynamic pricing management; 24/7 guest communication; cleaning coordination; maintenance coordination; key/lockbox management; owner reporting; and tax remittance. Some managers also include professional photography, direct booking website setup, and revenue consulting. Always ask what's included versus billed separately — cleaning fees, supply restocking, and maintenance are often additional.
Are there hidden fees in vacation rental management contracts? +
Yes — the most common hidden fees are setup fees ($200–$500 one-time), professional photography fees ($300–$600), credit card processing fees (2–3%), guest damage claim processing fees, maintenance call-out fees, deep cleaning fees billed beyond normal turnover, and early termination penalties. Always ask for a complete fee schedule before signing. The advertised percentage often excludes these add-ons, making the effective cost 5–10% higher than quoted.
Is it worth paying for vacation rental management in Bradenton or Anna Maria Island? +
For most owners, yes. Bradenton vacation rentals average $41,000–$51,000 per year in gross revenue. Professional management typically costs $9,000–$15,000 annually in fees, but offsets this through better pricing (average daily rate $301 in Bradenton), higher occupancy, and fewer vacancy gaps. Owners who switch from self-managing to professional management frequently report a net-positive outcome even after fees — plus the significant time savings. The ROI calculation gets stronger the farther you live from the property.
What is a fair management fee for Anna Maria Island vacation rentals? +
On Anna Maria Island, fair full-service management fees run 20–28% of gross revenue. The island commands premium nightly rates ($300–$600+ for waterfront homes), so even at 25%, the absolute dollar cost is high — which is why reviewing what's included matters. Companies charging below 18% are typically partial-service. Companies charging above 30% should offer demonstrably better marketing reach, occupancy rates, or revenue results to justify the premium.
How do I compare vacation rental management companies in Florida? +
Compare on five criteria: (1) fee transparency — ask for a full written schedule, not just the headline percentage; (2) what's included vs. extra; (3) local vs. national — local managers know the Manatee and Sarasota market, respond faster to issues, and build guest relationships that generate repeat bookings; (4) their current listing quality on Airbnb and VRBO — search for their properties and evaluate presentation; (5) owner reviews on Google or Trustpilot, not just guest reviews.
