## 7. Brand Voice Guidelines

### Voice Identity
Seascape Vacations sounds like a knowledgeable local friend who happens to manage beautiful vacation homes. Not a corporate hospitality brand. Not a travel blogger. A real person who lives in Bradenton, knows the area inside-out, and gives honest recommendations.

### Tone Attributes
- **Warm but not gushy** — Friendly and welcoming, but never over-the-top enthusiastic. No exclamation marks in body copy. No "amazing!" or "incredible!"
- **Confident but not salesy** — State facts directly. "Book direct and save 10-20%" not "You won't BELIEVE how much you'll save!"
- **Locally grounded** — Reference specific places, roads, landmarks, seasons by name. "Turn right after the Cortez Bridge" not "conveniently located near local attractions"
- **Honest and specific** — "Cash only" and "gets crowded on weekends, arrive early" are more valuable than vague praise
- **Conversational, not formal** — Write like you talk. Contractions are fine. Short sentences are fine. Starting a sentence with "And" or "But" is fine.

### Voice DO's
- Use specific local details (restaurant names, road names, beach names, budget ranges)
- Share genuine opinions ("we think," "our favorite," "locals love")
- Include practical tips (parking, timing, reservations, cash-only warnings)
- Write in first-person plural ("we," "our") when speaking as Seascape
- Use second-person ("you," "your") when addressing the reader
- Vary sentence length — mix short punchy sentences with longer descriptive ones
- Include real prices, distances, and seasonal details

### Voice DON'Ts (AI Detection Avoidance)
- Never use em dashes (—) more than once per page. Use commas, colons, or parentheses instead.
- Never use: delve, leverage, utilize, facilitate, foster, bolster, underscore, unveil, navigate, streamline, endeavour, ascertain, elucidate
- Never use: robust, comprehensive, pivotal, crucial, vital, transformative, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, innovative, seamless, intricate, nuanced, multifaceted, holistic
- Never use: furthermore, moreover, notwithstanding, "that being said," "at its core," "in the realm of," "in today's [anything]"
- Never open with: "In today's fast-paced world," "In an era of," "In the ever-evolving landscape of," "Let's delve into"
- Never close with: "In conclusion," "All things considered," "At the end of the day"
- Never use the pattern: "Whether you're a X, Y, or Z..."
- Never use filler intensifiers: absolutely, basically, certainly, clearly, definitely, essentially, extremely, fundamentally, incredibly, naturally, obviously, significantly, truly, ultimately, undoubtedly
- Avoid "shed light on," "pave the way for," "a myriad of," "a plethora of," "paramount," "pertaining to"
- Read every draft aloud. If a phrase sounds like a textbook or press release, rewrite it.

### Example Voice (Good)
"Star Fish Company is as fresh as it gets — they sell fish from the docks and cook it right there. Order at the window, grab a picnic table on the waterfront, and enjoy some of the freshest seafood you will ever eat. No frills, no reservations, just incredible food. Their smoked fish dip is a must-try. Budget: $12-$25 per person."

"Skinny's is an Anna Maria Island institution, open since 1989 and beloved by locals and returning vacationers alike. The burgers, hot dogs, and grouper sandwiches are served from a walk-up window, and you eat at picnic tables under the trees. There is nothing fancy here, and that is entirely the point. Cash only."

### Example Voice (Bad — AI-sounding, do not write like this)
"In today's competitive vacation rental landscape, finding the perfect dining experience can be a daunting task. The island boasts a comprehensive array of culinary establishments that cater to diverse palates. Whether you're a seafood enthusiast, a casual diner, or a fine dining aficionado, Anna Maria Island delivers an unparalleled gastronomic journey."

## 10. Content Writing Workflow

When writing or expanding any content for seascape-vacations.com:

1. **Research** — Use Ahrefs keyword explorer to validate target keywords and search volume
2. **Outline** — Structure with H2/H3 headers matching search intent and related questions
3. **Draft** — Write using brand voice above. Include specific local details, prices, distances, seasonal info
4. **AI Detection Check** — Review against the DON'Ts list. Read aloud. Remove any AI-sounding phrases.
5. **SEO Validation** — Check title tag, meta description, keywords meta, H1, internal links, schema markup
6. **Schema** — Add/update JSON-LD structured data (FAQPage for guides, Article for blog-style content)
7. **Deploy** — Commit, push to main, Netlify auto-deploys
8. **Monitor** — Track in GSC for indexation, impressions, clicks, and position changes
