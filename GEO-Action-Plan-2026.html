Your site has strong technical SEO foundations — schema markup, FAQ sections, AI crawler permissions are all in place. But two critical GEO gaps are killing your AI visibility:

Gap #1: Zero comparison tables across 71 pages

AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) heavily favor structured data they can parse and cite. Tables with pricing, features, distances, and ratings are the #1 content type that gets pulled into AI-generated answers. Your competitors don't have this either — first mover advantage is massive.

Gap #2: 85% of pages use identical H2 structure

60 out of 71 stays pages have the exact same 4 H2s: "Available Vacation Homes", "Frequently Asked Questions", "Related Vacation Stays", "Ready to Book Your Getaway?" — Google's helpful content system flags this as template/low-quality content. Each page needs unique, topic-specific headings.

The good news: your competitors (Airbnb, VRBO, Vacasa, TripAdvisor) dominate Google search results for Bradenton/AMI queries, but NONE of them are optimized for AI citation. When someone asks ChatGPT "best vacation rentals in Bradenton Florida" — nobody from this market is getting cited. That's your opening.

The GEO Strategy

Traditional SEO = rank on page 1 of Google. GEO = get cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews. For a low-DR site like yours, GEO might actually drive results faster than traditional SEO because AI engines don't weight domain authority the same way Google does. They weight structured data, factual specificity, and freshness.
